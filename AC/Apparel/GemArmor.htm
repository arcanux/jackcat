Combine as follows:

Shadows:
Dark Speck + Dark Speck = Dark Sliver
Dark Sliver + Dark Sliver = Dark Shard
Dark Shard + Dark Shard = Shadow Fragment

Fragments:
Cracked Shard + Cracked Shard = Tiny Shard
Tiny Shard + Tiny Shard = Small Shard
Small Shard + Small Shard = Crystal Fragment

Gems:
Dark Shard + Small Shard = Glimmering Gem
Shadow Fragment + Crystal Fragment = Sparkling Gem
Sparkling Gem + Sparkling Gem = Scintillating Gem.

Redeeming the Gems:
You can redeem the gems whenever you want. The better gems will yield higher level armor. You can redeem more than one gem. You will get different armor pieces the second time you use a gem. You can only get one set of each quality armor. If you try to give the smith a third gem of the same quality, he will keep it!! However, you can get a set of the same armor, but of different quality by redeeming a third or fourth gem. (Thanks to Caeryn Dryad at CoD for this tidbit.)

Note: This quest became repeatable during A Perfect Paradox and you can now get more than one set of the same type of armor!

Update Information

During the To Raise A Banner of Flame update in July, Shadow Armor changed again! The smiths were repeatedly murdered and are located at nearby lifestones. Their vitae has made them unable to produce the Shadow Armor at its former strength. To see stats of the Pre-Patch Shadow Armor, click here.

Adding Atlan Stones for Elemental Protection

The Taste of Twilight brought a new change to the Shadow Armor. Atlan Stones may be inserted for new elemental protections. Read more about this change in Aliester's Corollary. 

Neo-Shadow Armor

During the Fever Dreams event, the Armorers returned to their former health and were able to produce Shadow Armor at its former strength. This armor is commonly referred to as Neo-Shadow Armor and is a silvery hue, with a wield requirement of Level 30+.

Alean the Steel forger tells you, "Nuhmudira has finally restored us to full health! She has also stocked us with some extra supplies that we needed, such that we can provide you with either a version of the original Shadow Armor or its successor. If you wish to trade one version of your Celdon set for another, simply hand it to me. Please take out all Atlan Stones from your armor before handing it to me."

Alean the Steel Forger tells you, "Also, if you wish to color your new Greater Shadow Armor, you may buy a vat of color from my assistant. If you do not like the color, please give me the armor piece and I will replace it with the standard piece, which you may then choose to recolor."

Statik Shot of LC  reports the following:
"Players who turn in Glimmering or Sparkling Gems will now receive the original Lesser or Medium Shadowhunter Armor. Players who have the 'stoneable' or 'post-patch' Lesser or Medium Shadowhunter Armor will be able to trade it for the original version, and vice-versa.

Players who turn in Scintillating Gems will now receive armor very similar to the original Greater Shadowhunter Armor. The differences between this GS Armor and the original is the new armor is a different color (and players will have more color options come March), and that these GS pieces have a Wield_Requirement of level 30 (meaning you have to be at least level 30 to use the armor at all). Other than that, all stats are the same.

Players will also be able to trade the new Greater Shadow Armor pieces for the old 'stoneable' pieces and vice-versa. Currently, players with original Greater Shadow Armor are not able to trade in their original GS* for the 'stoneable' versions."

Adding Shards

During A Gathering of Colors, adventurers began finding shards on Crystal Shards. When used with Neo-Shadow Armor the shards increase the AL by 50 and add a Major Ward spell (see the table below for which shard adds which spell). The Armor also turns black in color with gold trim.

Neo-Shadow Armor with shards have a 0 Difficulty and the following protections: A/A/A/A/A/A/Ba. You must be level 50+ to wield.

Greater Shadow Amuli - AL:240
Greater Shadow Celdon - AL: 260
Greater Shadow Koujia - AL: 245

Once you use a shard, it is destroyed. Shards may be given and hooked. You may use different wards on a set of armor. For example, it's possible to have a Greater Shadow Celdon suit composed of legs with Major Flame Ward, Girth with Major Acid Ward, Breastplate with Major Frost Ward, and Sleeves with Major Storm Ward. At this time, there is no known way to reverse sharding GSx.

Dyeing Sharded Shadow Armor

During the Mirror, Mirror event, it became possible to dye Sharded Shadow Armor in 3 different colors. Visit Miko Li, Jojii Adherent in the Kara Pub at 83.3S, 47.2E and hand her vials of Lapyan, Hennacin or Relanim to receive dye suitable for use on the Sharded Armor. Visit the patch page for pictures of dyed armor!

Miko Li, Jojii Adherent tells you, "Though I try to live my life according to Jojii's path I sometimes find that I lack discipline. I have begun painting, and hope to continue doing so. I have noticed that many who come here seek the armor that is wrought by Koga Hideki and his peers."

Miko Li, Jojii Adherent tells you, "I wish to assist those who wish to bring the colors of the rainbow to the armor he makes. If you hand me vials of Lapyan, Hennacin and Relanim dyes, I will make them into paints that will stay upon the shadow armor that has been enhanced with the new magical shards."

Post Patch Shadow Armor is gold in color with reduced AL and can be used with Atlan Stones (stats are below). Sometimes referred to as old shadow armor, or stoneable Shadow Armor. When an Atlan Stone is inserted, the armor gains elemental protection and an additional 20+ to AL. Stoned armor may not be buffed. This armor may be dyed.

Koujia - AL 195 - -

If you give dyed Neo-GSx to one of the Smiths, you'll receive undyed armor back.

If you give undyed Neo-GSx to one of the Smiths, you'll receive Post Patch GSx in return.

If you give Post Patch GSx to one of the Smiths, you'll receive Neo-GSx in return.
