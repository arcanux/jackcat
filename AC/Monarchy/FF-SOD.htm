About the Shadows of Dereth

The Shadows of Dereth allegiance on the Frostfell server is based on a few simple principles: friendship, loyalty, devotion, teamwork, fairness, fun and honor. With a zero tolerance for combat macroing, experience chains, thieves and griefers, the monarchy was established by a small group of friends determined to keep the old school allegiance feeling alive. Members are encouraged to attend the monthly meetings to meet people and so they know that their voice is important to the group.

Allegiance Roles

Monarch: The Monarch guides the discussions during clan meetings. Issues are tweaked until the group comes to a consensus. The Monarch has no more power than any other allegiance member. His role is to help keep order and to help keep the allegiance running.

High Council Members: The High Council Members help run allegiance events and help with any problems that crop up. Like the Monarch, High Council Members have no more power than any other allegiance member, but they do get special recognition and thanks, plus pretty colors on the allegiance boards. :) Council Members are chosen by their desire to help make the allegiance a better place for everyone, or for good deeds and selfless acts.

Clan Members: The Clan Members, the Council Members and the Monarch are all equals. Nobody is considered better than anyone else no matter what :).

Corpse Recovery Team: Anyone who wishes may join the Corpse Recovery Team. Each team member carries an inscribed Bandit Shield (because they are rare on the server are unique looking) designating their role.

History of the Shadows of Dereth

The story of the Shadows of Dereth actually begins with the demise of Archer From Hell's allegiance. Like so many young wedded couples, Savona and Archer weren't able to weather the ups and downs of marriage. The union dissolved, and the monarchy soon followed.  Archer decided to create a new beginning in the form of Army of Darkness and swore to another clan. Over time, Army of Darkness made new friends and gained new followers. When Army of Darkness turned 50, Archer From Hell (now level 80) was retired.

Army of Darkness became deeply involved in this new group, attaining a position of importance. As his access to the inner circle increased, so too did his disagreements with the clan. Unsatisfied with big monarchy drama, Army consulted with close friends on what to do. The decision to leave coincided with the clan's decision to release him; Army of Darkness and his former clan mutually agreed to part ways.

Army of Darkness was now faced with a difficult choice: did he search out a new group to join, or should he go back to the role he played in previous days, as a Monarch. He discussed the choice with his followers, asking their advice. They agreed to strike out on their own, as equals. True equals, where no matter what level, rank, or number of followers, the only thing that matters is attitude, honor and the ability to get along with others.

The group is young, but thing are going well. They try to do quests every day, either a low level, mid-high level, or non-restricted quest, and then hunt together and hang out at the mansion chatting. Their goal is to minimize the drama, deal with issues right away, and not keep secrets from each other. Many are friends in real life, or have spoken on the phone. They include a real life photo gallery on the clan web site.

Tips For Monarchs From Army of Darkness

Army of Darkness offers these tips to Monarch based on his experiences:

1. Be nice to others. Be polite. Respect others and respect lines in dungeons.
2. Do what you feel in your heart is best for you and your crew.
3. Treat everyone fairly. Who cares about numbers, rank, followers... everyone should be treated fairly, and people will respect you if you do this.
4. Remember... don't try to take on too much yourself. You're only one man/woman. Try to get some of your allegiance to help you out with some stuff :). And try to do things like quests that involves the allegiance doing stuff together. Often it encourages teamwork, and it's a good way to get to know fellow allegiance members.
5. Offer people who try their best to make their allegiance a good place for everyone some way of getting recognition for their good deeds.
6. Never neglect any of your vassals or followers. Remember... a follower is the same thing as a vassal :). Every one is part of the same family. A family helps each other in times of need and they do things together.
7. Never give in to temptation. It may sound good to take in xp chains because you gain a lot of xp, but if you don't, people will respect you for standing up for what you believe and staying strong.
8. Never be too proud to admit when you're wrong. Hey, we're all human. Everyone makes mistakes. We're not perfect. Learn from your mistakes.
9. Learn from other allegiances. They might be able to teach you a few things about running your own allegiance. You might even be able to improve upon what they have (I know I have).

Members of The Shadows of Dereth Speak

I was hunting in Ayan BSD one winter's day in Dereth, when I came across a player named Army of Darkness. From that day, we have been the best of friends. After much pain and torture in previous clans due to people leaving, quests being in another time zone, people arguing, Army and his small group of followers decided to take Dereth on, as the clan "The Shadows of Dereth."

It's been going for a short while, I'm guessing 6 months or so. In those 6 months, I've never in my life seen so much determination from a group to make an allegiance work.

We run quests for the clan on a daily basis, whether it be for those of the family who aren't above a certain level or those where we all join in. The majority of the clan is European, although we do have a lot of Americans also.

I like Shadows of Dereth because it's a small, friendly group of people. The quests that take place are mostly in the GMT time zone, so that suits all the Europeans perfectly. We always have a lot of fun, whether it be talking at the family mansion or doing quests together. Its the best clan I've ever been in. I think the main reason for that is the people; we are all a fantastic group of people.
