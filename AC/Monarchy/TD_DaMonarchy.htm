About DaMonarchy

DaMonarchy kicks off the new Monarchies of Dereth section! Danier filled me in on a bit of the background of the origins of DaMonarchy and how his earlier experiences as a vassal and grand-vassal taught him valuable lessons about how he wanted to lead. How he went from a level 34 to level 45 in less than a week (back in the days before BSD!), killing the White Rabbit with ferocious intensity. How after weeks of struggle and research, he cracked the spell formula on his own. Then he adds, "Of course, Splitpea came out roughly 2 weeks later, had a rational user interface, and was instantly adopted by the public at large, but that's a mere detail! <g>" How he eventually broke to become a monarch, and how over time, his little group has grown from a handful of players to over 2000 people, not so much from active recruiting (with a few notable exceptions, such as Doogen), but through the attractive atmosphere of friendship and fun surrounding them.

Some of his insights follow:

"I learned that the primary relationship in the game was between patron and vassal, not follower and monarch."

"Specifically, I became convinced that the ROLE of a group was to BE a group. Yes, that might sound odd or strange, but I came to the conclusion that the job of a group was to simply be a community."

"But... back to the story... <But this IS the story Maggie... the relationships, the community, the feeling safe enough within a group to HELP people.. this IS the story.> "

"We made some mistakes, by which I really mean that I made some mistakes. Due to my distaste for the Aerlinthe Orgy of Ugliness, I kept the group far away from that quest until perhaps 4-5 months ago, when Doog replaced my hate-hate relationship with it with a love-hate. damn him! <g> I have been slow a time or two to eject a person.. and slow a time or two to actively recruit specific people who could add so much, but in the end that is all a wash really.

What we have done, and I want to be careful in how I express this sentiment, is to assemble a group where people can be relaxed and be free to simply be nice, to play the game, and to have someone with which to share themselves. Not that the members of DaMonarchy wouldn't be nice without the group, but that a large part of the intent I have with the group is to establish a culture and social setting where acting nice isn't something one does to get ahead, or to obtain something, and where being nice isn't rewarded with a bat to the face, or by use or abuse. The goal in many ways is simply to be able to have a place to play and a support group where people can have fun, can be as open and friendly as they care to be, and can be safe from being used in doing so.

Most of this is a mood / culture thing, and such things are very delicate and fragile. What makes it work isn't me spending vast amounts of time being accessible, or being willing to break off hunting to answer questions, or sacrificing my firstborn to Asheron or anything dramatic like that. Yes, I do spend a lot of time in /tells, and I do spend a very great deal of time leading, planning, running, organizing, scouting, and generally thinking about quests which are run for the benefit of other people rather than myself. I genuinely enjoy helping out by doing that sort of thing, and can have a great deal of fun running across the Dires to get some funky portal tie for a quest.

But what makes it work is that there is a large group out there who want this thing, this Monarchy, and who are willing to pay for it with their time, energy, emotion, and caring. While we sometimes have fights, misunderstandings, and disagreements, we all find there to be something special in what we have built, something worth trying to keep. How does it start? How DID it start? I have related the events above, but they really aren't of particular relevance when it comes right down to it.

It starts when one person heals another, tosses an armour spell, and says have a nice day, and when the person being healed decides that he would like to be in a group where people can just do that. Not for each other, just in general. That really is the long and the short of DaMonarchy, the group of which I am pleased and proud to be at the head."

Danier,
DaMonarch

In Asheron's Call, By Doogen of Rithwic
----------------------------------------------------------
In Asheron's Call, you may make a friend.
Sometimes friends wear Tangerine.
Friend might share a vision with you, of sharing, giving, teaching, and having fun.
Or he may just buff you when you've decided that 40% would end your call.
Sometimes the call is about leadership and growth through giving yourself away, and learning that in return you become more of a person when you give yourself away.

Or you may learn that even small archers can use a life wand.
You could run by a stranger in Glendon Wood, a little girl hunting rabbits and drudges.
And instead of saying hello, you can heal her, because we all are mages Zonata,
Some of us just deal with it better than others, right?
Then others become powerhouse archers dealing in life and magics easily as an archer nocks a bow.
Healing others with a laugh, a smile, and a giggle or watching a sunset.
In this call, you can find a new friend.

Or you can meet a friend who was there before you
And watch her fight as hard as someone can fight,
Despite not having the fastest connection to the 'net.
Congrats on the level Quietfire, I'm proud of you.
Sometimes the call is pure persistence and sticking to a goal

And some days you'll learn that someone is your friend already,
Even if you haven't met her yet.
Simply because Dacia is a friend - was your friend already,
the moment you joined the allegiance.
In the call you're simply accepted and embraced.

And a child can be your fellow-mate.
And a teenager can teach you something about yourself.
And you can have fun.
And learning the game can be a game.
And teaching the game within the game can be a game.
And dealing with others outside your group can be the game that you teach others to game.
And you can trade. And you lose. And you earn. And you give. And you laugh. Out loud. Rolling on the floor. Because the call IS tangible. It's a game-shaped experience that will never be stolen or grieved.

Because in Asheron's call you can decide in an October to learn a quest.
In the call you can learn how to lead a four-hour Adventure.
And you can talk a wisp killer they call Leslieo, a Milgan-shaped tank, and something they call a Wei...
Into learning that adventure with you.
Oh sure, they might think you know what you're doing.
Then six months later you can look back and see just how much fun you experienced.
Learning how to kill a Lady and an Ejan-shaped Smith.

Because Dereth is a world, and for part of my day, all the doogs are in it.
We don't know what a doog is, but we know... that Doogs ARE everywhere.
And Doogs are a collective
And Damonarchy is an allegiance of collectives, some of which are doog-shaped, whatever that is.

In Asheron's call you can join a thing when it's 110 big.
And you can become a leader when it hits 500 small.
And you can try to influence it with a thing like a chain at 1,500 wide.
And you can realize it's not built out of chains.
That it doesn't rely on lines or weak links.
Rather it's a mesh of people and things and events and doing quests every week for 100 weeks. It's not about exp, it's what happens while exp magically appears out of thin air. And it's big and small and tall and wide. It's a call.

It's a person and a collective and an allegiance and a quest and a trade and a loss and a friend and a buff and a guy named Buf. It's a Dumble who dores, a Way who showers, a talking-thing made of Shon, a house with twenty golden gromnie-cornerstones. It's a fellow and a shout to a Jack shaped cat. It's a hi and a hi ho, it's a sub-shaped hub. It's a journey to a pit that is ninety minutes to run or a bot that gets you there in a Roila shaped call. It's the Ender you all never met, the Griff who came back, an Exodus-shaped Andy, and a Furby hunting Kil Ho Gal gal drooling on a J-made acid quest that never really ends.

In my Asheron's Call, Damonarchy is the family in the game that's the pastime that is the call.

Hi Maggie! Why do I love Damonarchy? Well, probably because it's just the best monarchy in the whole game, that's why. Ok, WAIT ...I realize that was not a good way to put it, I mis-spoke, forgive me. Damonarchy is, er, a very good monarchy! They have organized hunts and quests, literally thousands of members, and there's always someone around to help retrieve a corpse or two in a nasty situation. Does that sound too enthusiastic? Let's just say, then, that they are not bad folks, ok?

I'm kidding, of course. I know you are a big fan of Damonarchy. After all, you were the one who introduced me to Danier, Damonarchy's monarch.

Actually, I suppose should mention that I am not formally a member of Damonarchy. I mean I'm not a member at all! As you know, I belong to YOUR monarchy, which is a very excellent one. I like your monarchy and always have liked it. You are a fine monarch. As your only vassal, may I say that things couldn't be better!

Damonarchy is a pretty good runner-up, however. They have been kind enough to make me a sort of Honorary Hanger-On, and I've had many fun and stimulating experiences with them over the years. Innumerable Dead Neds have been reclaimed through the force of Damonarchy arms and combat resources. They have school spirit.

In fact, as a total outsider, I really think I'm well qualified to recommend this monarchy. Here are their strengths:

* They spend an amazingly large amount of time PLAYING THE GAME, rather than standing around the lifestone talking trash. This characteristic is often overlooked when weighting the pros and cons of an allegiance, IMO. They play a lot, and they seem to LIKE IT. Enjoyable play beats the buggerbits out of tedious bad-tempered play.

* They have relatively few MEETINGS. The importance of this cannot be overemphasized.

* They have little interest in ASSIGNING BLAME, and not much skill in doing it, either. Such small blame as there might be is usually casually assigned to the leadership, who seem to accept it as the unavoidable consequence of competence and integrity. We could all learn from them. (The leadership, that is.)

* In situations where good preparation matters, such as dangerous quests, they are well-prepared. They understand that there is a knack to managing 30 players in a dungeon. Practice makes proficient.

* There are no Damonarchy members with serious unresolved psychological issues that impair game play! This is wonderful news. No bossy fussbudget know-it-alls, no mean-minded back stabbers, no pissy-ass peeps trying to compensate for the flawed parenting they endured as children. They may be a tad weird, but they show up at the dungeon ready for work and unburdened by dig-me baggage.

* Finally, their hearts are pretty much pure, and stout, and lion-like. They are valiant warriors, imbued with courage. They are tenacious, never giving up. They are loyal and true. They seem to care about one another. They are crafty, ingenious and generally wicked-smart. They have high quality equipment and frightening stats. In fact, Damonarchy may have the game's only level 100+ mule. With a 400 cooking stat, I believe.

That wraps it up, I think. Doogens Mage is a fine executive officer, always ready to pretend that my errors are normal and the sort of thing that could happen to anyone. Danier, the Monarch, is not quite able to go that far, but he is nice enough! He is also a fine military strategist, a knowledgable student of history, and he has 1839 Ned Cleversun corpses to his credit. That's got to count for a lot. Er, I think he may have picked up a few Jackcat corpses too!
