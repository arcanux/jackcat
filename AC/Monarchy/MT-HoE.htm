About The House of Elder
I asked Elder about the structure and philosophy of his monarchy. His answers were uncomplicated: no membership requirements, no experience chains, have fun, help others, go questing!

Could it really be that simple to run one of the largest monarchies on Dereth? Surely he'd need an army of generals to keep the ranks in line.

Quite the contrary. Instead of generals, the House of Elder has a group of volunteers who act as "advocates" for the family. The Council of Elder is a non-governing body, available to help allegiance members with urgent advice, allegiance policies (including policies on Player Killing) and more. The Council also keeps Elder informed about the current morale of the allegiance.

Members are urged to use the site message boards for non-urgent questions and to attempt to contact their Patron before asking the Council to step in. In a crisis situation, members may also make use of the Emergency Corpse Recovery Team.

Perhaps the philosophy of the allegiance can best be summed up in this signature of the Monarch:

Honor, Respect, Devotion to One Another.

Your servant,

Elder

Special Mentions

The House of Elder is home to at least two well-known players: Fist de Yuma, whom Elder calls a great natural leader, and William the Bat, quest leader extraordinaire. Fist writes the weekly column, Day in the Life of Fist de Yuma, published every Tuesday on The Olthoi's Lair. William the Bat is the creator of a fabulous screenshot repository: William the Bat's Action Screenshots!, which contains some of the best quest screenshots in existence.

      

The allegiance's Quest Schedule is maintained by Maskman and regularly scheduled quests are led by experienced players, such as Dark Angel of Soul.

The House of Elder has even been featured on on G4TV's (a television gaming network) cable network television Portal, hosted by David Meinstein. The segment showed in-game moving video footage of an allegiance gathering, a few fighting sequences, some shots in and around Tou-Tou, Nanto, Dires, a couple of Dungeons, and Kelci Mansion, as well as real life shots of Elder and his family.

The Story of Asha'man

I was a boy, like any other. I worked with my father in the shop, cutting oak planks for the wine barrels he'd make. Being a cooper's son had its advantages, of course. We always had meat on the table and I had the opportunity to meet many of the nobles when they summoned my father to bring them some nicely toasted barrels for their vintages.

It was during these trips that I learned that nobility has more to do with the way a man conducts himself than with who his parents are.

The Lord of our region was a spoiled, callous young man who'd never had to work in his life. His estates were enormous and had under the umbrella of peace for as long as anyone could remember. Lord Yale had every luxury imaginable and entertained himself with the distractions that only the well bred can afford. He was known far and wide for the exceptional falcons his aviary produced. He was also known for his unemotional cruelty.

I had just seen my 15th winter when my father received a missive from Lord Yale demanding twelve new casks, to be delivered before the mid spring celebration. If we worked feverishly, we would just have the time to complete the task. Father and I purchased the iron straps and the fine oak timbers. We split the wood and formed the barrels.

While toasting the inside of the barrels, I discovered that if I concentrated just right, I could seem to make the flames behave the way I chose. Father and I laughed in delight at the mystery and together we trained my abilities such that I could make the fires hot enough to quickly ruin the barrel, or cool enough to give a long, slow toasting. After the second week, I could even summon a bolt of fire and launch it at a distant target, though I was only really successful in doing this when I had a pocket full of onyx and some medicinal herbs.

Because of my newly discovered talents, the product was finished ahead of schedule and were, without doubt, the finest brandy casks we'd ever produced. Ever the professional, my father sent a messenger to Lord Yale's Spring Estate giving notice that we were delivering the barrels in the next couple of days. My spirits soared as we drove the wagon down the Kings highway. I couldn't wait to see the expression on the face of Lord Yale's master brewer when he saw the barrels.

We were surprised that Lord Yale, himself, met us as we pulled up in front of the manor. He was dressed all in dark blue velvet and had a cup hilt sword hanging from a gilded scabbard at his side. He appeared every bit the gentleman.

Lord Yale believed himself to be an excellent judge of the cooper tradestuffs and examined the barrels very closely. My father loved a good joke and so prompted the Lord to notice how smooth the boards were on the outside of the barrel. Lord Yale nodded approvingly and commented at how the fine sanding of the staves would help with the aging of the brandy. My father could not contain his mirth at this absurd statement. It was almost a whole minute before my father could compose himself to offer an apology for his fun.

Lord Yale was literally red with fury. He stormed over to the guard house and dragged the Captain of the Guard into the courtyard by his ear.

"Seize them all!", he bellowed. "Take him too.", he said, pointing at the poor man helping us to unload the wagon. "Throw them in the dungeons and I expect you to swallow the key."

In spite of the stories I'd heard about our Liege Lord, I never expected him to react so poorly to a good natured joke. I stood there in shock as the Captain called for his men.

"Run, Ash!", my Father yelled. "Don't worry about me."

I ran as fast as I could. As I reached the woods near the manor, I saw a stream. I'd heard enough stories from the Ranger in the pup to know that I should run up stream, in case dogs were brought to track me.

Hours passed, and I knew that I had escaped. The shame of my cowardice burned inside me. I wanted to return to the house and valiantly fight to free my father. I knew that I would die quickly if I tried any as stupid as that, but I grieved horribly. My flight had probably cost my father his life.

I stood in the stream, tears in my eyes and cursed the sun for rising that day. I wanted to swear revenge, but my Father had taught me that a life of revenge was empty and bitter. I swore, instead, to do more good than Lord Yale could do evil. I would make my mark in the world as an honorable and loyal vassal to a Lord who proved his worthiness by his actions and compassion.

It was then that a purplish portal opened before me. I knew, somehow, that this was the gateway to my future. I stepped through and found myself in a wood. On the far side was a beautiful maiden archmage, sneaking up to a floating creature, intent on the kill. I tried to throw my fire bolts at the creature, to aid the mystical woman, but fizzled miserably. I heard the monster whisper "Onrui Chon-Ter" as it pointed at the girl. She fell, clutching at her abdomen. In moments, she'd become a beast of burden, a poor dumb mule. I took the crest of her Lord, the mighty Zulu Dawn of the Eternal Circle, and set out to find my fortune and a worthy Master, with Sindy at my side.

-Asha'man
In memory of my Father.
Archeress, lvl 54
