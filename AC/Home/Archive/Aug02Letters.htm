Aug  2002 - Update to Nuhmudira's Boon

Dear Maggie,

On this page: (at the very bottom)

http://www.thejackcat.com/AC/Hobbies/Quests/Boon.htm

It is written:

"Note: even though the Storehouse guards say you can only enter once a week, you are actually able to go through as many times as you want."

A more correct assessment is:

The Storehouse Guards allow you entry for a period of one week, after which you must wait another week before you can enter again. So if you begin the quest on a Saturday, you have seven days during which you can enter the Storehouses as many times as you need to. On the second Saturday your timer expires and you will not be allowed in for yet another week. On the third Saturday you can gain entry again, for the next 7 day period.

Mishiko of SC


Hi Mishiko -
Ahhh. Excellent clarification!! Thanks very much for sending the information in. I will try to get the write-up updated soon!

- Maggie the Jackcat

Hi Hannibal -
I'm back! It's rather a tragic tale, but it has a happy ending. First, my business partner was out of town for a week, so I was just a little extra busy in the work arena. A staple attack, I fear, would have been no help. (But thanks for the offer! ;-) Then I had a wedding to go to. Then, well, I was in computer HELL!!

My system has been having... issues for several months. Frequent lock-ups, sluggishness, and just general bad behavior. The downward spiral began accelerating and I knew I had to do something fast. I decided the best solution was to wipe my hard drive, get rid of Win98 and do a fresh install of Windows 2000 professional. But since I've been kinda sloppy with back-ups and had almost 40GB of data on the drive and had procrastinated myself into a somewhat desperate situation, I decided the quickest easiest way to save all my data was to get a nice new larger zippier hard drive. (This excellent idea was actually my vassal's. He are smart!) I installed the new drive and copied all my data over before the wipe. Then I did the wipe, did the Windows install... and still had issues. Serious issues.

My computer-whiz brother-in-law diagnosed hardware problems: failed hard drive. Or maybe mother board! Whee! I disconnected the old drive, made the new drive the main hard drive and so far so good. I'm back up and running!

Ok, so it really wasn't *that* hellish. It could have been a LOT worse. It was just, as these computer things always are, time consuming and a pain in the behind, with brief moments of great panic and fright! It's not like I wasn't using my laptop or didn't have DSL at work. It's just that I prefer working on my main computer at home, surrounded by my wonderful 40GB of stuff.

Maggie the Jackcat
