August 26, 2001 - ***More info re: Ned's Spellcasting Essay!

Just wanted to drop a line on the spellcasting tips essay by Ned. Excellent article, but perhaps he doesn't know this, and it is SUPER helpful, so please pass the word to him for me!

When in war mode, the following are also valid commands:

ctrl-insert: go to Last spellbar (V)
ctrl-page up: go to First spellbar (I)
ctrl-delete: go to First spell, current spellbar
ctrl-page down: Go to Last spell, current spellbar

This creates opportunities to further organize spellbars. Needless to say, spellbars 1 and 5 are my most important spellbars in light of this.

I put 'urgent' spells at the beginning of a spellbar so that they can be instantly addressed by number (ie: Drain health is ALWAYS 7 for me). I put frequently used but 'not so urgent' spells at the end of a spellbar (ie: heal others, portals, non-mage related self buffs, true value) where they are instantly accessible no matter where I am on the current spellbar, with just a few keystrokes (as few as 2! ctrl-pagedown+end to cast the very last spell on the bar)

I'm sure there are aspects of this that I may be missing which some other brilliant mind out there may think up or even already know, so keep your thinking caps on!

Wryan
Solclaim
Mana Conversion Specialized 4 School

August 26, 2001 - Tips on Spellcasting Key Setups

I read Cleversun's new tips on spellcasting key setup with interest. However, I feel I must add my own 2 pyreals, because I feel I have a nice alternative. It is less intuitive than using the navigation keys, but here goes:

I reconfigured my AC to use other keys for movement. I prefer to have my left hand near the TAB key, and the right hand on the mouse. The left block of my "crooked" Natural Keyboard has these keys

1 2 3 4 5 6
Q W E R T
A S D F G
Z X C V B

Q W E are used for turn left, forward and turn right
A S D are for Step left, back, and step right
Z X  are used for sit, rise

Now I use R T for Id and Cast spell.
I use  F for prev spell and G for next spell.
I then use V for prev tab and B for next tab.

 That means I have spell tab navigation, casting and use of shortcut keys 1-6 at my left hand's fingertips. That is very efficient since it allows me to move around and cast all spells with one hand. Also you can move to the END or BEGINNING of any active spellbar by pushing CTRL+F or CTRL+G, and to spell bar 1 with CTRL+v and to spell bar 5 with CRTL+B.

I have put my recall to lifestone spell on tab 5, last position. In a jeopardy I can quickly recall by pushing CTRL-B, then CRTL-G and lastly T for casting the spell. This also allows you to put the recall command on a keyboardmacro, like I use my MS Strategic Commander for. Also it prevents you from accidentally casting Portal Recall while in a heated combat situation. I have done that a couple of times during a quest that I lead, because my recall was on shortcut 9. Quite embarrassing.

Immacolata
Wintersebb
Level 45 War Mage

Amendment to response: My clever vassal has solved the printing problem!! This is cool. All you need to do is upgrade your browser to IE 5.5. It has a built-in smart print preview option which allows you to format your page setup so that everything prints properly. Once you have Internet Explorer 5.5 installed go to File, then Page Setup on your Internet browser. If you change the left and the right hand margins to .4" then the page should display correctly. (Top and bottom margins can remain the same.) I also recommend going to Tools/Internet Options/Advanced and make sure that the "Print background colors and images" is not selected. Now go to your favorite page, choose the Print Preview option and voila! You should see a nice printable version of your favorite page. Pretty slick! And certainly a lot easier than redesigning the whole site! ;-)

Hi Zangsho Deathbringer -
Thanks very much for your kind words! I know very little about the ins and outs of Archery, so I asked Ned Cleversun if he would address your questions himself. His response is below.

- Maggie the Jackcat

----------------

Hello, Zangsho -- Thanks for writing. I'm glad you like the site; Maggie works hard on it and has done well. I remember when she put the first group of pages and tables and lists on the web -- to help me get around in the game -- and I must say, it has grown! She is a fine monarch.

I liked your letter so much I decided to turn the reply into a future essay for the "Sermons, Essays and Tips" feature. If you don't mind, I'll include your game name and server in the Essay and quote from parts of your letter. The answers to some of what you ask will run a bit long, and I thought there may be a LOT of archers out there who would like to refer to the answers. The info will be more accessible outside of the monthly letters section.

But I think you deserve some faster answers in exchange for such a fine letter, so here is an abbreviated reply.

> If you don't mind, I would like to know HOW Ned Shot a shadow for 606 pts damage?

The 606 was my personal best against an Umbris. As the infomercials on TV say: these results are not typical. A lot of factors came together. I have pretty good Life and Item magic. I start with Greater frog crotch arrows and a composite bow made from the finest materials crafted by specialists (not me!) with very high skill. Then I add BD7, HS7, Def7, and SK7. My working bow skill is 301, exactly. The resulting package is so horrible that many monsters turn and run when I bring the bow out. Before shooting this particular Umbris, I dropped Imperil 6 on it, followed by Blade Vulnerability 5.

Using this system, Umbris sometimes die with one shot and usually die in fewer than 4 or 5 shots. The trick, of course, is not to die yourself. That is not always easy!

> I am in a dungeon with Drudge Raveners by the dozen, all by myself, and am trying to find the best arrow to kill them with.

I have standardized on Greater frog crotch arrows, and use Greater Armor Piercing or Greater blunts for special situations. Greaters work better, enough better to make them worth whatever trouble it takes to get them. I avoid elemental arrows.

> are vulns really that big a difference in the damage that you do?

Vulns make a breathtaking difference. A good, solid Imperil can easily triple or quadruple the damage done by each shot. The problem is that it can be difficult to get past the resistance of any particular monster. Vulnerability spells and Imperils have created a lot of dead archers, let me tell you! Half of my deaths (currently over 1200) came while I was standing on the battlefield failing to Imperil a monster. It is a wretched, embarrassing way to die. But when you hit, ahh ...then they are toast.

> and how does a person throw a spell and switch to a bow in time to kill something? What am I missing here??? 

This is an excellent question. There is a trick to it, as you inferred there might be. Use the control key to switch between the wand and the bow, with each assigned a shortcut number. My bow has been in the #8 slot since I started, with my wand or orb in #1. Ctrl/8 brings up the bow straight from the wand.

But it is not instant, beware! It still takes a steady heart and strong nerves to stand firm while a monster is charging and attacking. Many times I've had to grit my teeth and tense my body to resist the visceral urge to break and run. It is a scary business. But if you do it right -- WHACK! You get some monster beat-up that is very cool.

- Ned Cleversun
