March 27, 2002 -  Paper Dolls

Hello Maggie

You host a fabulous page. Many thanks for your hard work.

I read in this patch update about the use of paper dolls to use mana stones upon. I searched and searched and could not find a walk through on where to get one of these new items. Might you point me & your many fans in the right direction? Thanks.

Footslow of Leafcull
Level 61 - 3 School Bow


Hi Footslow -
I meant to get this letter up sooner. I was amazed at how many people wrote to me, asking me how to get a Paper Doll. You actually already have one! The paper doll is a general gaming term for the picture of your character in your inventory screen (not you in the game world, fighting monsters, but the pic of you to the right of your screen, surrounded by your jewelry slots). All you have to do is double-click on your mana stone, then click on the pic of yourself! Thanks for the compliments, and I hope that helps! It's a great new feature!

- Maggie the Jackcat

It's a little easier for me now that I have so much awesome help, but still, it's been a tremendous amount of work over time. I think that if you keep at it, and more importantly, *enjoy* what you're doing, you'll build something that others will enjoy too! I look forward to seeing yours and many more amazing sites in the future!

March 27, 2002 -  AC2

I'm curious about your feelings about AC2. Will you be starting over on AC2, or sticking with ACprime. My daughter doesn't want to switch, but I'm a tech junkie.

It would also be interesting to know how many people will be sticking with ACprime vs. how many will be going on to greener pastures.

Yoshi Kwang of SC
Level 18 Vagabond


Hi Yoshi Kwang -
I can't possibly answer the 2nd question about how many people will leave AC for AC2. I can't even really answer the first yet. Here is what I suspect will happen, based on my previous experience of switching from Ultima Online to Asheron's Call. I played UO for quite a long time -- Grand Master Warrior, Grand Master Fletcher (I think those were my titles. It's *really* been a while). I had my house, my vendors (quite a successful little operation -- I sold regs, runes, XBows, Greater Heal Potions? I think. I can't *believe* I can't remember this stuff!). The game was starting to get to be a drag for me. I'd log on, restock my vendors, boost back up the skills that had decayed, then... I was out of time! (I *hated* the skill decay in UO). Since I actually do have very little free time, no matter what many of you who visit this site seem to think ;-), this was just no good. Where was the fun? Where was the adventure? Stock the store, boost the stats; stock the store, boost the stats. After a while, it felt like a JOB! And, really, I get enough work at work.

A friend of mine who first got me playing UO convinced me to sign up for some beta he heard about. I got in; he didn't. I'd send him email: OH MY GOD THIS GAME IS SO COOL!!! P.S. Can you pleeeze refresh my house for me??? I would alternate play days: one day in Britannia, the next in Dereth. Eventually, I found myself spending more and more time in Dereth, and less and less in Britannia. When I realized that I probably hadn't logged into UO in several months, I finally cancelled the account. But it was HARD! I was attached to my UO life. Sort of.

UO tried hard to get me back! They would send me emails, saying, your character has not yet been purged, it's not too late! Then: your character may be purged. Then: your character is gonna be purged, we are going to KILL YOU OFF FOREVER, but it's NOT TOO LATE, send money NOW! Agghhhh! I couldn't resist! Not my little UO sprite me! But, alas, it was not to be. It had been so long, my downloads and stuff were all outdated, and I couldn't log in properly. Faced with the pain-in-the-ass next step of having to seek out tech support, I said screw it. (Let that be a lesson to you, Zone! We find it hard to leave, but we should find it *easy* to come back! ;-)

Er... what was your question again? :-) Oh yes! Will I leave AC for AC2? Who knows! I suspect I will keep a foot in both worlds initially, and one of them will eventually win me over. I'm not at the point in AC that I was in UO. AC doesn't bore me. I think it's fun. I have stronger social ties in-game. I am probably more heavily invested in AC than I was in UO (the web site and all, plus my legion of adoring fans ;-). I think that I'll find it difficult, time-wise, to devote my attention to two different games long term. I'm sure a lot of my decision will be based on where my good friend plays. I could even end up in Norrath one day! (hee hee)

- Maggie the Jackcat

March 27, 2002 -  Hollow Armor

I've gotten quite a few letters asking what Hollow Armor is. They see people selling it in the Hub (Subway). What is it, they ask me. How do you do the quest? How do I get a Hollow Suit? But every last one of the letters I got checked the "do not publish" button! Doh!

Don't be embarrassed, letter writers! Turns out lots of people had the same question!

There is no Hollow Armor quest. Hollow Armor is armor that is worn to fight Hollow Minions. If you hear someone say, "wear your hollow armor on the quest," it's a signal to mages *not* to wear a buffed robe (hollow attacks ignore armor protects)! It can be any armor at all, but usually it refers to a very high base AL. There you go. End of mystery. Now where can I get my curtains? ;-)
