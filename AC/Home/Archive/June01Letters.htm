 June 18, 2001 - Niffis Info

The Niffis pearls are dropped by level 20 Glissnal Niffis and level 42 Opor Niffis. I personally believe I got my Small Niffis Shell off a Listris not Parfal. In all actuality they are probably dropped by both of those. I'd also like to point out that you may want to put the level of Sephal Niffis (60) so that an uninformed player doesn't venture out into the Dires and die. They have bad level 5 debuffs and an incredibly strong bludgeoning attack to boot. Just sharing my knowledge :)

Seldin the Rouge

June 18, 2001 - Update Information

Maggie, got some info for you, on the patch. First off, all your hard work on vender rates just got flushed... down with the merchants consortium! Newbie towns are buying from us at 90%, and selling to us at 135%... and that's just about the best rates you get. Eastham is at 90% and 155%, Kara at 170% and 80%, and the Dires is dreadful... 70% to sell there, not sure about what they charge you to buy. These rates are uniform at ALL venders, though I have not checked out roaming peddlers yet. Secondly, Niffis drop pearls and shells now, though I haven't managed to get anything to show you. And in tribute to a dearly departed author we all know and love.. or should...  you can now receive towels.

All Mud Golems drop muddy towels, which you can turn in for nice clean towels at your nearest society rep... it's quite the thing to wander around in our clean white undies with a towel wrapped around our waists. And these are not ordinary towels... they change color when paired with a girth! You can also obtain wet towels rarely too from water golems, but when I turned mine in I got the same clean towel as a muddy.. don't know what it takes to get a green towel, I just hope it's not a coral golem that's dropping it! ;P

Now here's the important info...

When I first logged on I despaired, for I saw that it seemed our dye plants had not changed. BUT - I have an unconfirmed rumor that one can turn in their golden gromnies for purple dye plants! Yes, PURPLE! I will try and get more info on where this happens, and get back to you as soon as I can.

June 15, 2001 - Ned's Hunting Tactics 

Well the patch was today, and while searching for something to do to avoid boredom during the download I decided to check your site. It seemed there was a new section, with tips and advice from your infamous vassal, Ned Cleversun. I read the latest tip [on archers hunting groups of monsters] and had to comment on the line about it being totally useless to melee fighters. This is far from true as while fighting in the field I often keep a stack of thrown weapons [often dropped by creatures I'm hunting at the time] for use in the tactic Ned described. They almost always evade the first attack [tw untrained], but they still register that it occurred and everyone comes looking for you. Return to the group and spend one more thrown weapon on that same critter and it'll aspire to be your best friend in the world while the others could care less. It's very useful to fight a single tusker, drudge, banderling, shadow, etc. etc. The same works with wands that shoot war bolts, bows and xbows. I prefer thrown weapons.

Giddy
LeafCull
Level 37 Swordfighter

June 15, 2001 - More On Ned's Hunting Tactics 
Hiya Maggie:

Nice feature on your vassal's hunting tips, and would like to make comment on his last tip about firing at one monster.

This is something an archer better learn, along with slide reloading, for survival. This tip also works for anyone with creature or life magic and using it to debuff the monsters.

The melee fighters out there can also do this by using a range weapon skill untrained. Sure, going to miss, but the important thing is to try to hit the monsters, even if they evade. I've also used a wand and done the "wand monkey" on a monster to lure it out of a group with a melee fighter.

Take care and look forward to seeing you again at the ACPL

Maddy

Hi Maddy!
Nice to hear from you again. This is somewhat of a duplicate letter, but it gives me a chance to plug the ACPL again and Ned Cleversun really likes the attention! ;) Thanks for the tip. Looking forward to seeing you in Boston!
