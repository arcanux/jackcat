February 28, 2002 -  Olthoi Queen and Forums?

Hi,
I started playing AC again after the expansion, and I meet up with a few friends and that started getting me back into it. I was looking at the quests in your quest page and I saw the Olthoi Queen (mainly the reward). I was wondering if there is a chance to do it by yourself, because 45million exp is a lot of exp, so I was wondering about that.

Could you also do a forum, because everyone goes to your website, and cause then everyone could share stuff and etc. quickly, instead of waiting for you to process the information and put it up, and people can sell stuff on it as well.

Anyway, kool site. It's hard to improve on perfection.

Ryu Sakoi
Leafcull
Sword + Life user


Hi Ryu Sakoi -
Glad you like the site! For the first question: no. You cannot do the Queen quest solo. Second, Maggie the Jackcat's Kick-Ass Fabulous AC Web Site will not have forums. I've actually answered this question many times before, but it's topical again and worth another look. My vassal often gives his opinion about the strengths of my site. For one, it has a unified voice. There's a coherence to the information because it's all filtered through one person (me). And the filtering process is an important one. There's a certain art to weeding through 2,000 emails each month and presenting the data in an entertaining, readily understandable fashion. (Ok, ok. I confess. I've only weeded through 1200 of the 2000 for Feb. and still have 800 to go! :)

Another strength is its positive spin. Let's face it: boards can be depressing! "Turbine SUXORS! The game SUX! YOU Suck! Blah, blah, blah." I feel that there are already ample opportunities for these viewpoints to be expressed on the web. I choose, instead, to celebrate the fun of the game, and the fun that players are having in the game. As my vassal likes to say, "Other sites have 'Community'; your site has players." Third, forums are extremely time consuming and require constant vigilance. Although the world is full of wonderful, engaging people, I'm familiar enough with the Internet to understand that the occasional butt-head who sends me anonymous hate mail because I once told them to quit stealing content from my site would not be able to resist stopping by for a visit. I'm really too busy to do the moderation forums would require. Plus, they're expensive. Huge bandwidth hogs.

So why then, am I giving the subject a second look here?

Because I've been considering whether to do an AC2 site, and in considering, thinking about what sort of site that would be, if I did one. Would I have a news page? Would I have forums? My vassal thinks that the only way forums would be workable would be for me to be totally non-involved in the administration. He says it's because he knows me. He knows me well enough to understand I would be unable to resist mixing it up on my own forums. And that I would be unable to act right! It's true. I would get into rip-roaring fights and arguments and flame fests and would ban everyone! NO mercy! He says the only way it would work would be if I would turned over full control to someone like... say... him! I know he's an old hand at this Internet thing, but I suspect this is just a blatant power play! ;-)

- Maggie the Jackcat

February 28, 2002 -  What Spells Should I Choose?

I have recently gotten level II Item spells what spells should I get? Someone mentioned lifestone recall, and portal recall. Why are these important?

Steve Ling
Frostfell
Level 18 Swashbuckler


Hi Steve Ling -
I've been getting lots and lots of mail from newbies since the Dark Majesty expansion was released. It's fun reading the questions! For a Swashbuckler, you'll probably want some spells to buff your weapon, like Blood Drinker, or Heart Seeker. And, yes, one of the main reasons people get Item Magic is so they can recall. These spells give you the ability to move around Dereth more easily. I'm lifestoned in Arwic, for example. When I cast lifestone recall, I am teleported to the Arwic lifestone, no matter where I cast the spell from. Out of my element in a dungeon, severely overburdened and surrounded by terrible creatures? Instead of dying, I can't just recall out to safety! Swoosh! Safe at the lifestone, without dying. It's very slick.

- Maggie the Jackcat

February 25, 2002 -  Newby Quest

Do you have information about the quest for new guys like me?

Lief Erickson
Harvestgain
Level 3 Mage
 

Hi Lief Erickson -
Newby (or newbie) is actually a nickname for a new arrival to the game -- a new player. There is no quest called the "newby quest." Instead, you'll often hear the starter quests referred to as "newbie quests" because you can only do them when you're levels 1-6 or maybe 1-9. After you get through the Training Academy, pay attention to the next direction you're given and that should get you started (they'll usually tell you to find another NCP to talk to - non player character). Good luck, and welcome to Dereth!

- Maggie the Jackcat
