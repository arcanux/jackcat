 May 22, 2001 - Correction & A Warning On Lugian Armor Quest 

Maggie,

After spend ALL weekend working on the Staff of the Mind quest for my patron, I found a few things in the Reign of Stone write up that are off a bit. The Lugain Sceptre is not 900 burden, but 4800 burden. It is a very heavy whacking stick.

And now for my warning:

Like I said I spent most of the weekend working on getting the Scepter of the Mind off the LT in the second Lugian camp. I think they call it a camp for a reason, cause there are tons of people there. I was there for a good bit on Saturday and finally the LT spawns and I get the kill, pissed a bunch off, but I did get it. I am a Level 50 Archer, but I do have Spear trained. My patron is a 3 School Mage, with War, Mana Conversion, and Arcane Specialized. I thought to myself, what a perfect gift!

I then proceed to the dungeon on my way to the Master smith for the exchange and then on to Rithwic for the Infusion potion. I am pretty pumped at this point, cause my patron knows nothing about this. I pour the potion on the Staff and I get the message, YOU HAVE DESTROYED THE STAFF......AHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHH. 

Why was it destroyed, is the skill check an actual number skill check? Does my Spear have to be really high? I have 211 base Spear because it is my secondary attack. From what I have read, the quest requires you to be trained in Spear when in fact you must have pretty high Spear to do it.

At this point I tell my patron what happens and off we head back to the camp to try again. Once again we wait a good amount of time with no spawn as the sequence has not fully taken place at the other camps. I get tired of waiting now and decide to log of and eat, and later 11:00 central time Sunday My RL wife and I log in back at the camp and within 15 minutes the LT spawn right next to me and I lay in, blasting him for 90 on the first arrow, 50 on the next. He didn't last long. I ID'd the corpse to see that in fact I got the kill. Once again I am pumped. I open the corpse and reach for the Scepter only to find out that it has a %^$#& timer on it. I thought I would pass this along to you so it does not happen to someone else. I have no clue what the timer is on the quest. 

All I can say is that I am very disappointed. If I knew I needed high Spear I would have buffed first before trying. I also want to state that I really think it is dumb to have a timer on a quest if you failed it. I can see if I got it right the first try and I could not do again. I understand that, because, otherwise, people would make a ton of them and trade them all day long. That was not the case; I failed and had to wait. That would be like having to wait to String a comp bow when you snapped the first string. It just doesn't seem right.

Thanks for listening to my whining,

Shimmerglow of LC

Thanks for all the info. I appreciate it. I haven't finished the actual quest write-up yet because I still don't have all the information. (obviously!) I've heard that some of the timers are only 24 hours. I hope that's the case for you as well. Good luck and let me know how it turns out!

- Maggie the Jackcat

May 21, 2001 - Dagger of Tikola Quest: Dull Dagger

A general warning: the 'Dull Dagger' is a cursed, no-drop item. If you pick one up you're stuck with 450 burden of useless junk. I think you'd then need an Archmage or perhaps a Healer to un-curse you so you can drop the thing, and that's incredibly expensive.

Changshi Li-Zan

Level 33 Blademaster

In general, if you want to get rid of no-drop items, you can give them to an NPC (like a Collector) to rid yourself of the burden. Hope that helps!

May 8, 2001 - Alchemy / Cooking Information for Dyeing 

You mention on the new dye page that there is always a chance of failure for the process, but my experience is that this is not so.

From where someone is 50% reliable to where they are 98%, there seems to be about a 20-30 point range. After that, the skill remains at ~98% for a very long time, perhaps 75 to 100 points, then it actually does become 100%.

Sugardaddy has not failed in about 3 months, since his skill got above 390, and he does a great deal of dyeing. If there is something you know which I do not, would love to hear it. (380 is listed as a break point on the page, but if you got this number from me, I must have miscommunicated; the magic number is 390.)

FYI, dyeing shirts, robes, shoes, etc., anything without AL is basically a freebie. Worst case, you mess up the color and have to do it again. There is never a magic damaging or negative AL problem, so no real reason to take such items to the best possible.

Not sure you ever explicitly mentioned it, but there is never any adverse effect on the magic of an item.

Loved the explanation on the armor types. If there is anything you need, or any experiments you need run, give me a holler. I can round up 50 plants and dye up a storm with no real problem.

Dan
