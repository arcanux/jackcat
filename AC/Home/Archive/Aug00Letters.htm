August 31, 2000 - The experience "Bug" in "New Stuff" Page

Ned has given you more xp than you have earned because those are raw
numbers, unadjusted for your leadership and his loyalty. If both scores were above 100 for the duration of the vassalage, then the numbers displayed would be low. Since we both know that those scores are well below 100... well... it explains the discrepency. Regardless, there DOES seem to be a bug in that the xp listed is not what was earned for the patron in question, but appears to be the xp given to patrons in general over the life of a character. Just thought you would like to know.

Danier
Thistledown
Level 62 Mage 

Hi Danier -

Thanks for trying to clear up the mystery. I must admit that even with your explanation, I still don't quite understand how the experience works between Vassals and Patrons. Ned Cleversun and I have, of course, an unusual situation, as he is now 18 levels above me. My Leadership stat is still very low, but he has, thanks to your merciless goading (in a completely loving way, of course :) and his high regard for his Patron, continued to beef up his Loyalty whenever possible. I believe right now he is concentrating on increasing his bow stat, to give himself a vitae cushion for his composite bow, so Loyalty has hovered around 125 for quite some time now. I'd love for someone to lay out a really detailed explanation of how experience gets distributed in an allegiance. If you run across one, let me know!

- Maggie

August 31, 2000 - Lockpicking in the Green Mire Grave

In the Grave you don't need to gather the keys to recover the armor or the yari, IF you have lockpick above 120. I have personally verified this and checked it many times. So if you want to screw up the fun and get there fast before everyone else, simply wait for the wisp to spawn, and rush the chest with your picks ready. But you better hurry 'cause that wisp is a pain when he gets behind and starts picking on you.

Hsaru Iron Fist
Solclaim
Level 28 Warrior-Mage

You're right! That would ruin all the fun! :) Although I must admit that I used lock pick to get to the lower level of the West Wing the first time I went to the Grave. August 31, 2000 - That Snowman

Look, I know this is probably rather stupid but is that REALLY a snowman in that picture, and if so, where did you find it?

Trennan
Solclaim
Level 11 Blademaster


Hi Trennan -

Yes, that's really a Snowman! They actually were all over the place during the Sudden Season. There were Good Snowmen (Level 2) who cast beneficial spells on you, and Bad Snowmen (Level 8)! There was even the dreaded Giant Snowman (Level 26),When you kill them, they sometimes leave carrots and snowballs as loot. Once The Great Work, deep in Frore, was defeated, warmth returned to the land and the snowmen all but disappeared. However, you can still find a few scattered about Dereth.
