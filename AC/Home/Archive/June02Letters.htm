June 28, 2002 - Elementals Everywhere!

Dear Maggie,

You probably already know this, but just in case no one has noticed it with all the new quests, the elementals are out and about in lower level areas. I took my vassal out to a place near Hebian where I trained when I was lower level, like ten or so. Well, we're doing fine, until we get to Golems. I barely manage to heal my vassal in time, because I'm an idiot and am nearly out of mana. Well, before you know it, I i.d. a creature I've never heard of before... what the heck's a Mox, I think. I look at it, level 56! Here?!? I must have been standing too close, or maybe I ran up to see what the heck it was, but I had forgotten that I'm running around with all my mana consuming items off... and without the right quarrels.... So, of course I died.

I came back with lightning quarrels, ready for that darned Mox, and couldn't find it anywhere. (That Mox must like peek-a-boo.) Later, that same day, I'm taking my vassal up to the Mossy Fort in Hebian-to, and right there in front is a scintilla, which I kill with slashing (didn't have acid quarrels with me). Ok, today I try to take my vassal to the Zombie House in Hebian-to, and well, I'm off course a little bit... (anyone who knows me knows I'm directionally challenged). Right there is a nice Scintilla with a little lightning elemental with it. I think it was called Static or something similar.

So, if you've already read any other accounts of these surprise party elementals, that's all good and fine, you can disregard my letter :). If not, feel free to edit as you see fit. After all, you're awesome.

Dichroic
Frostfell
Level 43 Life/Xbow Chick


Hi Dichroic -
I have, indeed, gotten many letters about the encroaching elementals. I don't know what's up with that, but I'm sure we'll find out!

- Maggie the Jackcat

June 28, 2002 - Into the Fathomless Chasm

Pcmorris here again. Well, being the action-starved quester that I am, I brought my friend, Muten Roshisama, with my archer, Morris the Haze, to the Devex level of the Fathomless Chasm (we did the whole quest in several hours with nothing but 2 level 29's). Your previous informant probably saw more than I did, as I didn't see any Frosts until I hit the only real jump in the entire dungeon. The place was child's play until we hit the jump. The Frosts and Shivvers like to buff themselves with Fire protection, so my fire arrows did a bit less damage to them whenever one of them buffed, but I simply switched to some frog crotch arrows I was carrying (all arrows are greater only, I didn't manage to hit 230 bow before becoming dangerously close to being too high).

The last section came up pretty unexpectedly (I was expecting the place to be quicker than Clough, but not this much quicker), and we came upon the main Frosts a little unexpectedly. We were pushed back twice to the main drop (it's a much bigger room than the one you get in Clough), but managed to clear them all. When the time came to meet the main golem, I realized I forgot to ask for a focus buff or a monster attunement buff... oh well, at least I got the golem's name and level.

The sad part of this whole trip was the boss... The Glacial Golem Margrave, a level 42 golem. I ran up and tried to ID it with no luck. Muten landed an imperil on it... it walked toward him... I turned... fired a single greater fire arrow into it's back... and... watched as this golem, who we had spent 5-6 minutes trying to clear dozens of frosts to reach... dropped like a sack of potatoes in a matter of seconds... from just the one arrow.... I didn't find out how much health it had, but after being imperiled, a level 29 archer, using greater fire arrows and buffed with level VII's, dropped it in a single shot.

I know, the place is supposed to be a lot easier than Clough, but mercy... that was so anti-climatic that I still can't get over it....

I really would like to go back down and get his stats for you, but... having Muten help to clear all those Frosts and getting nothing else for the effort seems like such a waste... although if anyone 'else' was with me to pick up 'their' decanters, I'd jump right on it. I really don't think it'd be very healthy for me to try soloing the dungeon. I would likely be killed by the frosts before I could get close enough to Margrave for the ID'ing shots (but apparently I'll have no problem running up to him and wiping the floor with him to clean them out).

-Pcmorris / Morris the Haze
Questing Lunatic of Morningthaw
