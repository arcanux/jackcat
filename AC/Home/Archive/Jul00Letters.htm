July 11, 2000 - About the Composite Arms...

I have discovered the following about Composite Arms. There are four possible arms that one can make. They are:

1. Fire Auroch Horn + BLUE colored Sinew (regualr or small). Shows up as a white horn with grey cuts.

2. Fire Auroch Horn + Cured Large Sinew. Shows up as a white horn with blue cuts.

3. Mattekar Horn + BLUE colored sinew. Shows up as an orange horn with grey cuts.

4. Mattekar Horn + Cured Large Sinew. Shows up as an orange horn with blue cuts.

That is what I have found so far. For some reason, the Auroch horn shows up as white (?) and the Mattekar horn shows up as orange. The blue sinews (regular and small) show up as grey cuts into the horn, and the Large sinews (orange) show up as blue cuts into the horn. That is what I have found from pictures and my own experience in making composite arms.

Orthanc
Leafcull
X-Bow Unarmed

Hi Orthanc -

Thanks for sending the info in! I'm still working on compiling the stats and the biggest unknown right now is the Melee defense. So far, I pretty sure that the type of Horn used for the Arms determines the Melee Bonus. If you're correct that there are four types of Arms, and if the Arms follow the pattern of the other components, giving three possilbe outcomes (the better of the lesser pari equaling the lesser of the better pair), then my best guess at this point is as follows:

#1 produces +2% melee
#2 and #3 produce +4% Melee
#4 produces +6% Melee.

Thanks again for the information! Please keep me posted if you discover more!

Maggie the Jackcat

[Update: It has been confirmed that the horns do determine Melee Defense. A Fire Auroch Horn (from a Bull only) yields +2% and a Mattekar Horn yields +6%.]

July 5, 2000 - Deceptive merchants!  Help!

Maggie, your site is an inspiration to me.  So many young adventurers willingly sacrifice fashion for added protection at the expense of everyone else's eysight.  I love to see the fashion showcased on your site.

I was wondering if you could help me with a fashion problem I've been having.  I'm attempting to assemble some outfits, but the shopkeepers keep fooling me!  They'll show me a nice white pair of pants, but when I buy them and try them on, they look dirty and grey!  Or they'll show me a nice dark blue hat, but when I buy it and put it on it looks purple!  I try the clothes on right there in the shop's fitting room, but the shopkeeper always insists that I pay up front, and he never lets me exchange my clothes for a full refund!  I keep the receipt and everything!  First they misrepresent the color of their clothing and then they won't even give me my money back. It's so dishonest! 

I have the same trouble when I find clothes and armor in the dungeons.  I dislike trying on clothes in the dungeons (no privacy!) so I often end up carrying around hideous garments for a long time before I can try them on and see how they look. 

Can you offer me any advice?  Do the merchants have some secret color-mapping code that I need to know, like "hot pink = purple" or something like that?  Is there somebody I can report these crooks to?

Thanks Maggie,

Nura al-Qadibi
Harvestgain
Level 4 Sorceress

Hi Nura al-Qadibi -

Your complaint is a common one, and one I've made myself many a time. I'm afraid there's just no easy answer. Dereth is, in many respects a wild and ungovered land. As far as I can tell, there's noone in charge. Which suits me fine, because that means *I* can assume the mantle of authority just as well as the next person.

The merchants will not change their ways. They'll sell you some green looking leggings and when you get them on they'll be orange. Your complaints fall on deaf ears. They'll insist you made a good buy! Your best bet, if you're looking for items of a particular hue, is to trade with your fellow players. But if you do, be cautious. If you think the shops are full of crooks, just wait 'til you get to Arwic!

Use common sense when trading and don't hand your AL: 184 Amuli Leggings to a Level 1 player named Kl3p+0. Even consider using a 3rd party high level player to complete your transaction. Good luck with outfit! And thanks for the nice comments on the site!

- Maggie the Jackcat
