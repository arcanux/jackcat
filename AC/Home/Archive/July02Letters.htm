I have not noticed any density changes, either on Vesayen near Cathedral or in the South Dires near the Emissary.

What I have noticed, however, are two changes:

1) The respawn rate for critters on the Vesayen Isles seemed to be much higher, especially for the undead. July, 2002 - Summing up Isparian Weapons

I've completed my tests on the Shimmering Isparian Bow. It buffs to 30 Bonus Damage (Blood Drinker VII on a normal bow gives you 22 BD) which accounts for many people doing much higher damage.

However, it does NOT do "extra" damage to single-elemental monsters or physical monsters; rather the damage bonus reflects the higher damage many people are seeing.

The Bow does, along with the other Shimmering Isparians, the bonus 300% damage to the dual and triple elemental monsters, such as those found on the Quiddity Ingot and Decanter of Essence quests.

All in all, it is a wonderful bow that is well balanced and now is my primary choice in battle!

Thanks!

Templar Nocturne, Leafcull


Hi Templar Nocturne -
Thanks for your on-going analysis of the new Isparian weapons!

July, 2002 - Mule?

I have no problem creating a mule, but how do I give him/her my stuff? (Stupid question I know.) I just don't know "how" it works. Thanks.

Cyrriss of WE
 

Hi Cyrriss -
If you create a 2nd character to hold your extra stuff, you can mule in one of several ways. If you own a house or residential quarters with a storage chest, give your mule storage access, put your item in the chest, log out, then log on with your mule to retrieve the item.

If you don't have storage, you can do one of two things. You can either put the item on the ground where you mule is logged out, then log out and log your mule in to pick it up, or you can ask a trusted 3rd party to hold the item for you until you can log your mule on. Both these methods involve risk. The item on the ground could decay or could be picked up by someone else who happens to see it lying there unattended. If you choose the wrong person to hold your stuff, they may not give it back. You can minimize your risk by muling in a secluded, out of the way location, or by choosing a trusted friend to help you. Good luck!

- Maggie the Jackcat
