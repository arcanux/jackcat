April 7, 2000 - Allegiance Break Penalty

Hiya yet again!   You're so good at answering, I thought I'd torment you again!  However; I am not tormenting you out of sheer laziness.  I've looked for this info on AC Vault, AC Stratics, Crossroads, Asheron's Lore and a plethora of misc sites and have yet to find the answer.

Dilemna: My hubby, as a newbie, pledged allegiance to someone who made a poor patron for his class.  He broke off the alliance about 3 levels ago.  We conned (hehe) my patron into taking him in.  Unfortunately, when he tried to pledge to him, he got a message stating that he didn't have enough experience to pledge (or something to that effect).  He swears he had 1K experience to go immediately after breaking with the first patron and now it says he needs 4K more to go. Humm...

I posted it to a board in similar wording and got some responses that attempted to explain, but I'm still really lost. So...do you know anything about experience penalties and how they work?  Is he doomed or merely bugged?

Your tormenting but devoted fan,
Jaide of the Quasi-Matching Hunter Green

Hi Jaide!
I will see if I can answer this for you. Yes, when a vassal breaks from a patron, there is a penalty for him to pledge to a new patron. He has to suffer somehow for breaking his oath of loyalty! Your husband's penalty started as 1K experience points. But the longer he goes without reswearing, the more the penalty will increase. Your husband was unable to swear to a new patron because he does not have enough unused experience points to pay the penalty.

When you fight stuff, kill things, you gain exp points. Some are applied directly to the skills you are using (i.e. sword skill), but there is a certain percentage that remains unused and you may choose to place them where you will -- to your skills or attributes. For instance, you may choose to raise you endurance, or coordination by spending some of these unused experience points on them. Your husband needs to get out there and kill, but not apply the experience to anything until he has enough built up to pay his penalty and to reswear to someone. Then, if the patron agrees, have him ask the Patron to RELEASE him. If the Patron releases him, he is not penalized. He will be a free agent again, without a penalty. The penalty only applies when a vassal breaks from the Patron. Hope that answered you!

- Maggie the Jackcat
