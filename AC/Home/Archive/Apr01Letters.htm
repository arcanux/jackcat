April 27, 2001 - A Strange Obsidian Shard

Dearest Maggie,

I have been long from home, traveling the central desert.  As you know, these lands are overrun with the strangest dolls. Though they seem innocuous, they are anything but...

The other day, I found a strange Obsidian Shard in the packs of one of these Innocent Dolls. It appears to be a chunk of polished volcanic rock. When I study it, there seems to be movement just under the surface. The most eerie thing, however, is that I have felt a strange pressure in the back of my mind since I picked up this item.

I took my find to the Obsidian Enchantress, certain that she could help me identify the source of this stone. Unfortunately, the evil witch kept it for herself, ostensibly for "further study". I've since obtained another of these strange items, but my experiments have been to no avail -- I am unable to use it.

I've considered taking this treasure to a trophy hunter, but fear that he will not reward me for my efforts. I am beginning to distrust the shopkeepers and crafters... could it be that the stone is getting to me?

I write to you for advice and guidance.  Have other travelers found these strange items or have you heard any rumors from the locals near you? My sorrow grows as I search the world for clues, and anxiously await your response, hopeful that you have information that will free me from the shard's allure.

-= Aspidistra Verte 
Thistledown
Level 31 / Dagger


Hello Aspidistra Verte -
Caution is indeed a wise approach when dealing with anything Virindi. One only has to read the chilling tales of Sir Candeth Martine to realize that a gentle tug on one's mind is reason enough to flee in terror!

The Obsidian Shard is not what it seems. First found on a Virindi in the bottom of the Gredaline Consulate 61.5S, 29.6E (Restricted to Level 25+), it was taken to Zaikhal to Diyas al-Yat, the Virindi Translator. The resulting item is a piece of lore: Edicts of the Singularity.

For more information on the new events in Dereth during A Reign of Stone, you can visit my patch page here.

- Maggie the Jackcat

April 27, 2001 - Vitae...And Some Praise For Your Site

Hi Maggie the Jackcat,

I must say that you have a website that is just as addictive as the game itself. =D Bravo.  I've been playing for about three weeks and just recently started reading your website and all I can seem to put together is, "I Love This Place." The way you categorized the site... all the tips and quests... and my current Favorite is the Runway. Glad to see I'm not the only one who likes to look good while keeping Dereth safe. =D

Before I go off on a tangent...

Easter morning I was off hunting in Qalaba'r between The Lugian Oupost and The Lugian Cit when I... how do I phrase this.... Let's just say that after 15 minutes there were several of me in different states of undress laying around the same area. =P I got some help and retrieved all my bodies and was off to the Lugian Outpost to work off My Vitae. Once inside I was ok... but then there I was again 10 minutes later.... Looked like nap time at a Nursery School. My bodies all over the ground looking so peaceful and then *blam* one more for nap time.

I discovered something that I'm not sure your readers may not know. Your Vitae tops out at 40% and that's as high as it gets. You keep losing items and pyreals of course, but you will not get a Vitae of more than 40%... trust me I know. =P I was killed three more times while at 40% and it didn't increase at all. I eventually got everything back... just thought I'd share the information... I'm not sure how many of us have been killed that many
times!

Uling Bayani
Harvestgain
Level 23  Adventurer / Jack-of-all-Trades

P.S. - Your site helped me to find my friend a wedding dress. Thanks =D

Hi Uling Bayani -
Thank you for the nice words! The Runway has always been very popular. I've simply got to update it more! Thanks for also passing along your hard earned knowledge. ;-) I've never gotten beyond 20% or so myself, but I know my vassal has deep intimate knowledge of the limits of vitae!

Just as an additional note, in case you weren't aware of it, you will eventually stop dropping bodies if you have died too many times in a row. Your loot and pyreal will fall helplessly to the ground. It's a good idea, if you're on a one of these spirals of death, to make sure to retrieve your corpses to avoid further disaster!

- Maggie the Jackcat
