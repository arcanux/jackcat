November 29, 2004
Off the western shore of the Direlands a soft pulse of energy forms. The energy steadily grows until it crashes into the ocean waters and reveals a partially exhumed temple in the sands.
An assault of mana pounds the western surf and then becomes silent. Residents at Ayan Baqur are taken aback at the appearance of a spectral being.
Thanks to tawni ii for reporting! See the patch page for details!

November 28, 2004
I hope everyone in the U.S.A. had a good Thanksgiving! I know I did. *burp*

The Bronze Statues quest has finally been cleaned up into a full write-up. The stats for the Statues themselves were also updated with the more recent changes.

Today's Blast From the Past is a tribute to my wonderful vassal, Ned Cleversun. When he turned level 80, I posted this tribute site. Mayhem ensued!

Update: A 5th King has entered Dereth! Thanks to Zerono of HG!
Kivik Lir's temple is briefly surrounded in a corona of magical energy. Deep within the temple another Burun King has crossed the vast divide!"
See the patch page for details.

November 23, 2004
Another Burun King enters Dereth:
In the depths of Hizk Ri's temple, a great magic is unleashed. Waves of mana pound the surrounding area, as another Burun King has forced his way onto Dereth!
Thanks to Darius of the Nexus from HG and Avail of LC! See the patch page for details!

It's another exciting Blast from the Past, this time of Ian Wilmoth's Break-Dancing Olthoi! It's a classic!

November 2, 2004
Five years ago, new portals opened from Ispar into Dereth and a wave of adventurers arrived. Since that time we have overcome many challenges and seen our society grow and prosper. In celebration of that day and in honor of the heroes who have found glory and riches, the Council has decreed that fireworks shall be set off at the bronze statues in the cities of Dereth. Happy Birthday, Asheron's Call!

It's election day in the U.S. Please make sure you find time to vote today! If you're one of those annoying people  who claim there's no real difference between the parties so it doesn't really matter, you're indulging in intellectual laziness!! Plus, you drive me crazy. If you're still undecided, feel free to email me, and I'd be happy to try and persuade you. ;-)

Don't know where to vote? Visit My Polling Place or call 1-866-MYVOTE1. Bring a picture ID with you as a precaution. If you're not yet registered, some states (like mine) let you register on the spot. Otherwise, you can talk to an Election Protection person if there's one available or call 1-866-OUR-VOTE for instructions. If neither of these works, ask for a provisional ballot (bring your picture ID). For other election resources, visit the League of Women's Voters site.

...ahem... Go Kerry!
