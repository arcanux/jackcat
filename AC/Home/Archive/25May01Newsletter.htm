AC SKINS
--------------------------
My last newsletter featured some of the new 3rd party add-ons for AC that have become popular. This month we have AC Skins! You may have seen screenshots on various fan sites that look, well, different from your own user interface (UI): a mysterious purpley-black background, one entirely in shades of grey. A "skin" is a way to change the appearance of a site, a program, AC's UI, without changing the content or functionality in any way. 

Installing an AC Skin is not for the faint at heart. Make sure you scan for viruses before downloading anything to your own computer. And make SURE you back up your portal.dat file before attempting this. As always, use at your own risk! Damien Sarin of the Aerlinthe Report fame and creator of the Olthoi Horde skin gives these quick and easy instructions for backing up this important file:

Create a new folder inside your Asherons Call directory and call it "backup". Next, COPY your 'portal.dat' file into the 'backup' directory. 

That's it. Quick and easy. 

So if you find you do not like the new skin, simply copy the portal.dat from your 'backup' directory back into your Ashetrons Call directory. It will overwrite the skinned one. And your game will be back to normal.

AC SITES IN THE NEWS
-----------------------------------------------------
Dizz's Diary of Dereth
http://www.worldzone.net/games/dizzarian/
Heard references to a battlemage (BM) on the boards but just not sure what one is? Check out Dizz's Diary of Dereth. This site will tell you everything you need to know about being a battlemage. There's a great starter guide with template suggestions, armor recommendations and leveling spots. You'll find a good resource center with links to great essays and spell information. Best of all, in addition to Dizzarian's own tales of adventure, you'll also find the journals of several other BMs. It's a treasure trove of useful information!

NeenerNeener.net
http://www.neenerneener.net/default.asp
Kwip's site, NeenerNeener.net, first came to my attention when I received some fan mail. The writer raved about my Jackcat site and said it was his very very favorite! That is, he added, right after NeenerNeener.net. ;-) With such a recommendation, I had to check it out for myself! Since then, I've become a regular, enjoying Kwip's offbeat brand of humor and boundless energy. His Ninja story alone made me a fan for life! Recently Kwip unveiled version 2.0 of NeenerNeener.net -- now with 50% more chum! For a hilarious look at the world of Dereth, be sure to stop by and check it out!
