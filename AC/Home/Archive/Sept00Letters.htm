September 28, 2000 - More on Getting to Ayan Baqur!

Several players wrote to give supporting information about the letter Ned Cleversun wrote, mentioning The Cave which he culled from Doc Feelgood's item in AC Explorer. Looks like they've confirmed its existence. Here are a few of the letters. Thanks Maddy and Pigroast!

Besides the way Danier mentioned, there is another way if someone else is already there.

Outside of town, just a couple of mintues, is a dungeon. The outside of the dungeon has Tumeroks, but ashes, gold wasps, and other nasties spawn around the dungeon. If you go inside (more Tumeroks), you can tie to the exit portal. You can then go and summon for your friends. Just make sure everyone hits the ground running when they go through the portal. :)

Maddy
Frostfell

September 12, 2000 - Traveling to Ayan Baqur!

Getting to AB is a bit nasty now, by design I take it.
Choices:

1.  Run from Black Hills / Dungeon of Corpses / Disaster Maze area.
2.  Take the portal at the bottom of Sylsfear or Carved Caves to the SW Dires lifestone near the Embissary, then portal to the Halls of Lost Light, running from there.
3.  Portal to Nexus, then run south along the shore to Ayan Baqur.. yuck.
4.  Take the Mayoi to South Dires portal.. yuck.

Basically, if you are looking for a fast and or easy to do it, forget it. Of all the stuff mentioned here, the safest is Sylsfear / Carved Caves to the SW lifestone, then to HoLL, then run north almost to the Lightning Vault before turning west and hitting AB.

If I have not mentioned a dungeon etc, then just assume it is no-tie, I have mentioned the BEST ways I know of, we run quests through there all the time, and this is the best we can do with it.

Danier
Thistledown
Level 63 Mage

September 3, 2000 - Crying (or How do I get to Ayan Baqur?)

LOL... How do i get to AB? I can't tie there anymore.

Sean M.
Frostfell
Level 20 Archer

Hi Sean -

I deferred your question to my wonderful vassal, Ned Cleversun. He writes the following:

"It used to be that the ONLY way to reach Ayan Baqur was to run from the South Direlands portal (62.2S 77.1E start point near mayoi, drops out at 82.2S 34.4W in South Direlands). That's how I reached Ayan Baqur originally, and it wasn't too bad. It was also possible, and still is, to run from a portal in the disaster maze area, but it was always a long and dangerous haul.
 
Looking in AC Explorer, there may now be a couple of alternatives, but I haven't checked them out. Using the "route" function, I routed to Ayan Baqur from Mayoi and got a very interesting route, but it still required a 25 click run from a portal on the rim of the Obsidian Plain.
 
However, I discovered one other very interesting item buried in the depths of AC Explorer. It DID NOT show up in the routing functions, so it is suspect, but there was a personal note from a well-known player (Dr. Feelgood) in the AC Explorer database. There is a dungeon North of Hall of Metos and Black Spawn Den, called The Cave. It is located at 47.8S 83.7W and is exactly 12.81 clicks North of Ayan Baqur. Here is a copy/paste of the note from Dr. Feelgood: This is the start of Sunshine's Way: a shorter route to Ayan Baqur than running in from the South Direland Coast or Disaster Maze area.   -- Doc Feelgood 6/6/2000
 
That suggests to me that the dungeon can be tied. If it can, then it's the closest portal to Ayan Baqur, by far. BUT ...you need to GET there first, hahaha. Right now I'd say that the shortest publicly available route is:
Mayoi to South Direlands (62.2S,77.1E) 4.94km
South Direlands to Slaughterhouse (80.6S,37.4W) 4.69km
Obsidian Rim Portal (5.5N,87.5W) 9.46km
Ayan Baqur (59.9S,87.9W) 25.81km
 
Hope all this helps!"

Shortly afterward he wrote to me again with the following:

"By the way, I did a sort of informal poll at Ayan Baqur, amongst the layabouts and gallants standing around in town. The consensus seems to be that Nexus (40.1S 79.2W) is the best place to tie for Ayan Baqur. Nexus is the place, Nexus, Nexus, Nexus. But Nexus requires a run of over 21 clicks of hard ground. I think this "Sunshine Way" (The Cave) is a better deal, being less than 12 clicks away."

Good luck!

- Maggie the Jackcat
