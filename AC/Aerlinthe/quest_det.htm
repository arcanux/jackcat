Ok, starting from the very beginning. Two Emissaries from Aerlinthe appear in Dereth. One in Chalicmere Fortress (17.7s 57.4w), and one at a group of temples in South Dires. (87.6s 65.6w). (The same temples that now house the remains of the Sand King Anadil.)

Upon entering Chalicmere Fortress, you will see Faladha the Emissary and hear these messages broadcast to the area:

----The dead man turns and looks down the rotted remains of his nose at you. A cool, Aristocatic presence invades your mind, and seems to speak in Roulean. "You are foolish to come here, outlander. This is the castle called Chalicmere, fortress of the Latzimestal faction..."
----"Ah, but such subteties are beyond you. To your barbarian eye, we are merely corpses and bones, all alike and monstrous. Yet it is not so, and these rotting forms you revile shaped the destinies of men and kingdoms far beyond your ken."
----"Obstruct me not, whelp. I have urgent traffic with the Lords of the Latzimestal..., Unless you will give me a note worth 10,000 pyreal. Then I shale grant you all the adventure you desire, if you be mighty enough to use my passage. Yet I shale not suffer barbarians in my home, and will prevent you from conjuring the gate yourself."

When you hand the Emissary a C note, he then gives you this message and summons a portal to the drop point on Aerlinthe (84.1n 47.2e). See the "Aerlinthe Map" in the menu.

----"The voice in your mind sneers, "You are wise to offer me such a boon, lest I strike ye down like the cur in the street. I shale open the path to my home, the ancient port of Aerlinthe. You would be wise to tread lightly, lest you wake the ire of my island my Lady."

A similar situation with the other Emissary in the South Dires, Garaena the Emissary,  except her fee is 50k. When you speak to her, you see this message:

----The rotting woman inclines her head stiffly, and a disembodied voice seeps across your mind like oil. "Child, you walk among the tombs of the Sand Kings. Long ago they were mighty captains in service to the powers behind the Ice Throne. Now they rest, and dream of new plots. Yet still they hold themselves great power, and are ageless and fey."
----"My mistress sent me hither to solicit their aid in a great cause. Perhaps you could serve, for I shall call the enemy of mine enemy friend. Those in shadow...thralls of the one called Slayer of Hope...beset our isle. If you give me a note worth 50,000 of the Yalaini pyreal, to fund my mission to bribe the Sand Kings, I shall open the gate for you.
----"Be warned; only the mightiest may pass through this gate, and I will not allow you to tie to it."

When you hand her the trade note you hear this message right before she summons a portal to the same drop point on Aerlinthe as Faladha's portal:

----The voice in your mind purrs, "Ai, this will serve. Seek the dark ones and destroy them...yet meddle not with the devices on the island of Aerlinthe. Perilous they are to those who do not understand their art."

All significant landmarks on the island are marked on the "Aerlinthe Map" that you can find in the menu on the left. In the section of the island where the temples are, or the "abandoned town" as people call it, inside one of the temples with a library, a "charred book" was found. The book was translated into the "Aerlinthe Record" by the translator in Cragstone library. A transcript of that text is linked at the top of the page. This text details the history of Aerlinthe and it's inhabitants.

Refer to entry RE 886 in the record, you find that Ejan was the second governor of Aerlinthe and also a Master Smith.

Upon further exploration of the "abandoned town" you will come across Smith Ejan himself in undead form. When slain, his corpse will have a "singed note" on it. The note gets translated into "Smith Note" in Cragstone. The note is as follows:

From: Ejan Aralea, Master Smith of Aerlinthe

To: Shaura Lemaih, Master Smith of Ireth Lassel

-

Greetings, old friend. May this find you well.

I have sent this messenger to you to inquire if you might have a certain spare part for the forging mechanisms in your possession. Tenkarrdun has been grumbley lately. You've probably heard, as I've heard that your Seral Ipharsis (Translator's note: literally,"Great Crown Besnowed") remains quiescent. Lucky woman! The mountains here may be hotter and more efficient forges, but they move with unsettling ease.

Yes, I fear we've had more pieces of the forges be crushed or melted by fresh flows in the last few days. Specifically, I need a new set of bellows for the upper forging mechanism, up here in the caldera of Tenkarrdun. If you think that's bad, I also have to get a golem to replace the bars on one of the lower magma sluices. Hard to get at, those are - we'll actually have to send it through our reservoir and into a thermal fumarole at its bottom! Fortunately, we have a couple of those bars stashed away in our storerooms here. We'll have to fix that before we can get to work on the forge.

I would normally send to Knorr for the bellows, but, well... Lord Asheron appears to have sealed the portals as his end. It's anyone's guess why. Some problem with his students, mayhaps. At any rate, I apologize for needing to plead poverty to you, Shaura. As soon as Knorr is open to travel once more, I will request replacement of your bellows.

Walk with light upon your shoulders.

Ejan

  The above note is the hint or lead that begins the quest.

- This entry in the Record describes in part some of what Ejan was saying in the note about the problems at the forge:

TE 463: Massive collapse of the Tenkarrdun forge tunnels during a landquake. Four workers are crushed, another sixteen trapped and suffocated. The entire lower quarter of the foundry is cut off. Shifting rock makes reopening the tunnels too risky, and Ejan decides to build a new forge works further up the mountain.

- The very last entry in the Record also describes what Ejan was referring to in the note:

HE 459: Severe landquakes cause magma flows in the upper forge tunnels. Smiths remove to the port while golems effect repairs.

In the note Ejan mentions that before he can work on the forge he needs to have a golem replace a bar on one of the lower magma sluices. (sluice: n. an artificial waterway with agate or other device to control the flow and level of the water). He mentions that he has some of these bars stored away in his storerooms. So that is our first goal... retrieve the "Sluice Gate Bar".

The "Sluice Gate Bar" is found in a lower section of the "Tenkarrdum Foundry" dungeon (88.9n 42.7e). I don't have any maps done of the upper part of this dungeon yet, only the lower section.  The bar is in the forge room on level 4 that has a number of the same ovens that were at the Crater and a lever. Check the map for details.

Now that you have the "Sluice Gate Bar" you need to take it to the Repair Golem NPC in the "Aerlinthe Reservoir Dungeon". (85.5n 43.4e). The Golem is deep in the bowels of the Reservoir. The dungeon is split into 2 parts, the second being 'The Old Ring" section which is where you will find the Golem. Following this partial map to get to the Olthoi Section, then using Nodokas maps of the Olthoi section, you will be able to reach the portal to the "Old Ring" section. The jumps indicated in my top floor Res map lead to the second floor of the Olthoi section, where you see Nodoka has indicated an entrance. You should be able to follow his maps down to the portal at the bottom of the 5th floor of the Olthoi section. In a pit on the 5th floor is a second Aerlinthe Reservoir Portal but it doesn't take you back to the beginning like you would think it does, instead it drops you into the Old Ring section. Using the maps, you should be able to make your way to the jump in the west end of the ring section.. You need to be careful that you jump on an angle that will land you on the safety gate or else you will fall deep into the magma pit and either die from the heat dmg, or get mauled by the golems down there. If you make the jump, you will see the Repair Golem. It looks like a regular magma golem. You hand it the "Sluice Gate Bar" and this message is broadcast to anyone in the area. "The golem turns and fuses the bar into the Sluice Gate. Somewhere below, you hear a grinding vibration through the ground... The fires of Tenkarrdun have been redirected the the ancient Yalaini Forges." . Once this message is broadcast, the golem crumbles to the ground and disappears, thus completing this stage of the quest.

Ok, now onto the next objective. In the note he mentions a bellows. "...Specifically, I need a new set of bellows for the upper forging mechanism, up here in the caldera of Tenkarrdun..." . With this months patch, people found "Pyreal Forge Bellows" up in the Crater Caves dungeon (66.7 N, 12.5 E) , in the bottom room, laying between a few of the oven/forge looking things.

After you retrieve the bellows, you head back over the "Tenkkardum Foundry".  The forges you need to get to are below the storeroom level. To get into that area takes a bit of a trick. The door is opened by a lever on level 3 in the elevated dome room. You need to assign someone in your team to wait up there and hit the lever when you are all at the door ready to go in. Once you work your way down to the forge, you'll see the walls are lined with more of the ovens found in the Crater and in the Storeroom of the Foundry. In the back of the room you'll find the Smithing Golem. You hand him the "Pyreal Forge Bellows" and a server wide message is broadcast. "The ground trembles beneath you! Mount Tenkarrdum has become active once again! On Dereth, the volcanoes echo the eruption on far Aerlinthe Island.". After this message is broadcast, the golem crumbles to the ground then disappears, and the forge begins to fill up with lava. This completes the next stage of the quest.

Next up is the "Behemoth of Tenkarrdum" Battle. There was no in-game hint about this aside from the volcano erupting. Since nobody   knew what to do after the server wide message, the only option was to search the whole island to see what had changed. The first thing that was apparent was the population of Hellfires and Plasmas in the caldera of Tenkarrdum had increased. Upon deeper exploration of the caldera, the Behemoth of Tenkarrdum was uncovered. It was nearly in the very center of the volcano surrounded by an army of Hellfires, Plasma Golems, Magma Golems, Firestorms, Fire Wisps, Embers... all kinds of ugliness. Also found was a large air vent spewing mist high into the air. You can see a scale comparison here.

The Behemoth resembled an oversized magma golem and seemed to be 100% resistant to War magic. Almost every initial attempt to attack Behemoth near his spawn point failed. A combination of lag due to the insane amount of monsters in the area and coupled with he amount of dmg they can inflict made the idea of killing this creature at his spawn point impossible. To give you an idea of what you are up against, a Hellfire is the top of the Fire Elemental food chain. If you take an Inferno and double it, you have a Firestorm. Take a Firestorm and double it you have a Hellfire. Plamsa Golems are the top of the Golem food chain it seems. I think it's safe to assume they are about double a diamond golem in terms of difficulty. They attack with lightning/bludgeon damage. White Pynthos Wasps attack with cold damage. Think Gold Wasp, then double that and you have your White Pynthos Wasp. Now, add into the mix the flares, embers, flammas, all the little fire elemental guys... easy to kill but there's a bunch so that added to the lag.  And that should give you a pretty good idea of what the Tenkarrdum caldera used to be like. Since the initial attacks, the particle effect on the fire elementals was reduced making the lag alot more bearable. Now questers are able to single out and destroy the Behemoth relativley quickly if they are well organised. (Tip: When you arrive to your striking point, most people have thier team log out. This make's all mobs who were trailing you as you entered the caldera break pursuit and return to spawn. This will reduce any unneccesary lag and make the attack on Behemoth alot easier.)

The initial idea was to lure it out of the center there somehow. Eventually 'safe spots' were found, which weren't very safe but were not as dangerous as the rest. If you could lure the Behemoth to one of these spots... and keep it there killing all the other things that might tag along... enough so that you have the Behemoth alone, then you have the chance to kill it. this tactic remined the same for the most part up till now, with the exception of the log out trick which eliminates the need to kill straggler mobs. When you defeat the Behemoth, an area wide message would be broadcast. "The Behemoth roars at the sky, perhaps cursing it's opponent *insert name of player who inflicted the most damage here*, then explodes into an enormous pile of flaming rubble.". On the corpse, a "Black Boulder" was found.

The "Black Boulder" is the reward for getting to this point in the quest. Based on speculation that it is similar to the "Black Rock" and "Black Stone" , the Boulder was turned into a Stone Collector. The collector gave back the "Black Fire Atlan Stone" which could be inserted in your existing Atlan weapons similar to any of the Elemental Stones. The stone enchants the weapon with these spells: Weapon Mastery Other 4, Blood Drinker 6, Swift Killer 4, Cold Protection Other 4, and Curse Of Black Fire. The Curse is a -50 to your Magic Defense. Difficulty is 180 and your skill in that weapon must be 250+ to operate the magic. Click here for a pic of the Atlan Staff with the stone embedded.

At this point, questers were not sure what to do next. Then a summary of the events was scribed which had a passage related to the island. From "The Story Thus Far"

The biggest news of the month was the discovery of a passage to a heretofore-unknown island. A pair of undead heralds arrived in the Direlands, one in the northeast at the undead fortress of Chalicmere, and the other in the southeast, at a trio of sandy old crypts. In exchange for huge sums of money, these emissaries would cast a portal to the island of Aerlinthe, lying to the northeast of Dereth.

Aerlinthe was an intensely volcanic island, with several peaks ringing a central lagoon. It seemed, in fact, that the island's nature had been the death of its original population long ago; a ruined port was discovered along the southeast shore, its inhabitants frozen into positions of horror by falling ash. Many fossilized undead skeletons still wandered the island, as did a host of bizarre new creatures. Coral golems lined the navigation channel into the lagoon. Tenuous vapor and plasma golems roamed the calderas of the volcanoes and the underground.

Intrepid adventurers, following instructions found on the bones of an undead Empyrean smith, managed to restart the old forge mechanisms built into the central mountain of Tenkarrdun. With a rumble that could be heard as far away as Ayan Baqur, the volcano came to life. Plumes of magma and a hoard of powerful blue-white fire elementals issued forth from the caldera of great Tenkarrdun. Those who visited the steaming crater found themselves overwhelmed and driven back.

However, the sighting of a great beast brought them back for more. The “Behemoth of Tenkarrdun,” a huge and powerful (though stupid) magma golem, had crawled up from the depths of the mountain to blister and squash all who dared approach its home. It was, eventually, overwhelmed and killed. Some say a mere duo managed to slay it, others insist that it took an assault of nearly a hundred to bring it down.

Unfortunately, Aerlinthe has managed to keep its remaining secrets for the time being. As Leafdawning drew to a close, the bulk of the invading force of fire elementals still held the caldera of Mount Tenkarrdun. While many searched unsuccessfully for some obscure, hidden method to end their reign, few seemed willing to directly challenge them in open combat. Perhaps this was due to the fierce conditions in the crater, or perhaps because what commanded the occupying forces could not easily be discerned.

Based on the parts that are in bold print, questers decided to plan another large scale assualt on the fire elementals in the caldera at Tenkarrdun.

A direct assault proved to be the key, as the forces attacked the fire elementals head on, eventually killing the one who was controlling them. This elusive Hellfire was hiding amongst the others, and has no distinguishable features to set him out from the rest. (Initially it was 2 levels higher than the rest, so it was easier to pick out, but that changed with the following prop) . Eventually after battling all the creatures found in the center of the caldera, they managed to find and vanquish the leader. When it was slain this message was broadcast to the area: As *insert name of player who inflicted most dmg here* smites the Hellfire, the flame-creatures sigh, and begin to dissapear back into the vents and fumaroles they surfaced through. But, from the base of the mountains slopes, you hear the whisper of falling ash, and the clacking of petrified bones...

When the Hellfire was slain, it dropped the Red Fire Infusion.

Shortly after the invasion was repelled at Tenkarrdun, reports started coming in about a second invading force that had erupted out of Mt Espar. The whole caldera there has become infested with infernos, flammas, magma golems and flares. Somewhere in the middle off all of them stood a lone Firestorm. This was obviously the figurehead of the invasion. The defending forces targeted firepower on it and managed to slay it, at which point this message was broadcast to the area: As *insert name of player who inflicted the most dmg here* smites the Firestorm, the flame-creatures sigh, and begin to dissapear back into the submerged vents and fumaroles they surfaced through. The lake of Mt Espar is peaceful again.

When the Firestorm was slain, it dropped the White Fire Infusion.

The next logical step was to check the remaining Dereth volcano, Mt Lethe. Lo and behold it was also overrun with fire elementals. Firestorms, magmas, flammas, infernos and all the like. After scouting the area, the lone Hellfire was spotted overlooking the action from high up on the volcano wall. Attackers managed to lure it down off the wall and quickly slay it at which point this message was broadcast to the area: As *insert name of player who inflicted most dmg here* smites the Hellfire, the subterranean creatures sigh, and begin to dissapear back into the vents and fumaroles they came up  through. Mount Lethe is peaceful again...or at least as peaceful as it ever gets.

When the Hellfire was slain, it dropped the Blue Fire Infusion.

The infusions need only to be applied to the SoLL in your inventory and they add the bonuses. Here is a screenshot of a SoLL buffed with the red and white infusions. And another shot.

With the 3 invasions having been quelled, the questers fell back to the message given at the Assualt on Tenkarrdun, specifically this part:   "But, from the base of the mountains slopes, you hear the whisper of falling ash, and the clacking of petrified bones... " . Upon searching the island, they discovered that the Abandoned Town where Smith Ejan resides had been overrun by Relic Bones and Firestorms in addition to the usual residents. As you can see in this pic, the town was packed with an obscene amount of invaders, just look at the radar. A vicous battle for control of the town took place, and eventually, like the other 3 invasions, a single non-unique Relic Bone was found to be the leader and slain at which point this message was broadcast to the area: Just before *insert name of player who inflicted the most dmg here* scatters the petrified bones across the ash of the port, it attempts to cast a portal to retreat! The ancient port of Aerlinthe is peaceful again... or at least as peaceful as it ever gets. But where does the portal lead? Surely it will not last long!

On the corpse of this Relic Bone, the attackers found the "Ring Of The WatchMan", a magic ring that casts Deception Mastery 4 and Person Attunement 4.

The portal the Relic Bone summoned while trying to retreat leads to Aerfalle Keep. This resets the entire quest.

Aerfalle Keep is a very unwelcoming place. Lady Aerfalle seems to be a scientist of sorts running some kind of labratory. The Keep itself is full of alot of eye candy, like the lab room with the Olthoi Larvae and the study with the olthoi statues, where you can also see a number of corpses hanging above a pit of lava, almost as if they were cooking. Also, there is a oversized Umbris Shadow in what resembles a prison cell. It does not attack, can't be spoken to or given anything. It's purpose is yet to be revealed. Deep within the bowels of the keep, the questers found Lady Aerfalle herself, donning a stunning black robe. They managed to defeat her, and on her remains they found the Staff of Aerfalle, Aerfalles Letter and a key. They key opens the Lady Of Aelinthe Chest they found upstairs. The chest harboured a whole treasure chest full of items, including both the coveted Aerfalle's Pallium and a complete surprise in Leikotho's sword "Ashbane".
