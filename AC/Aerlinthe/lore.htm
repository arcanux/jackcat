About the Old Ring

From The Aerlinthe Record:

RE 222: Loritane sends sixteen great ocean-galleons and two skyships to the island, carrying the supplies necessary to build a mining outpost. Lord Loritane and Lady Raina accompany the vessels in Tinoriel. The navigation channel into the lagoon is dredged. Reservoir dug to collect rainwater. All local water sources tainted with sulphur and ash. An Old Ring, buried under ash and ejecta, is found during the digging. It is left undisturbed, but considered a good omen. The island is titled Aerlinthe by Lord Loritane, a name which came to him in a dream. The central volcano is known of old to be called Tenkarrdun, Dericost for "Stoic Blood." The smaller peaks to the northwest and northeast are found to have been named Cunara ("Steaming Heights") and Ernassen ("Land Reflected"), respectively. The lagoon is dubbed Raina's Harbor.

So because of the loose wording you can't assume it means 'ring' like a Menhir ring or 'ring' like jewelry... but then we get this from from Garaena the Emissary :

"My mistress sent me hither to solicit their aid in a great cause. Perhaps you could serve, for I shall call the enemy of mine enemy friend. Those in shadow...thralls of the one called Slayer of Hope...beset our isle. If you give me a note worth 50,000 of the Yalaini pyreal, to fund my mission to bribe the Sand Kings, I shall open the gate for you.
The voice in your mind purrs, "Ai, this will serve. Seek the dark ones and destroy them...yet meddle not with the devices on the island of Aerlinthe. Perilous they are to those who do not understand their art."

So we hear that the 'mistress' wants some shadows slain... again the wording was somewhat loose... "those in shadow" not specifically 'those shadows'... but assuming she meant the shadows then we get this from Smith Ejan in the town:

Smith Ejan says, "Suvani hasode, Metresa, tamaa Der Hallae."
In your mind, the dusty voice of Ejan whispers, "Yes. The Shadows remain below, Mistress, at the Old Ring."

Ok... tie it all together and it makes sense... look at the map of the second part of the Reservoir... it is a magma section deep in the volcano somewhere... it opens into cavern that encircles the drop point room, shadows all over it and standing stones... loosely placed all in a huge circle. The rough map illustrates the formation ... this resembles a Menhir ring type formation hence the obvious conclusion is this is the 'Old Ring" mentioned in the text. We killed all the shadows and nothing special happened. No special items, nothing. Seems this whole section was just to lead to the Repair Golem in an earlier step in the quest.

About the Gromnietross aerie

From the Aerlinthe Record:

RE 274: The Blessing of Aerlinthe. A gromnatross makes her aerie on the peaks of Cunara. She is named Aurlanaa by the people of the port.

(Aerie; n. A nest, usually on a high crag or mountainside, of a bird of prey.)
Cunara is the peak to the west of Tenkarrdun as mentioned in entry RE 222 of the Aerlinthe Record. If you go up on top of this peak, you find an area that resembles a nest. Inside the nest is a handful of Ash Gromnies. Nowhere else on the island do you find these. When in the aerie, the immediate area is blinded by lightning every few seconds and you can hear voices similar to what you hear when standing on mana pools in meeting halls. Within the limits of the game engine, I believe this was Turbines way of confirming for us that Gromnies are in fact young gromnietross. Some people have mentioned things such as "If they are gromnitross, where are their wings? Why don't we have any gromnietross? Where are they all coming from? etc etc"...
If you look closely, Gromnies do in fact have tiny wings as you can see in this diagram. As for where are the Gromnietross... where are the grown up gromnies etc etc... well Turbine would probably explain that much the same way they explain about human children. "They are just off-camera". From what I gather, the current engine does not allow for flying enemies. If you examine Zefirs and wasps, the models have lower half, like invisible poles holding them up which is why when you examine a wasp or zefir corpse the cursor covers the size of a human instead of the clump of monster on the ground. They are just the same as panum or umbris, walking on the ground but the lower half invisible. So instead of leaving it out completely, I think they added this to throw us a bone. After seeing all the interest in the gromnietross on the boards by the lore people etc.

Next mention in the record:

RE 895: Aurlanaa leaves her aerie on the peak of Cunara. She meets several other gromnatross south of Aerlinthe, and they ascend, disappearing into the heavens. No gromnatross are seen on Auberean for twenty-two years. Severe lightning-storms and strange glows come over the southeast waters. The population is evacuated to the west. Darkness falls upon the shores of Ireth Lassel. A vast reach of Black Madness and fifteen Thorns accompany the Hopeslayer, his generals, and their army. Darkness defeated. The Hopeslayer is entrapped in the mage council's array. The explosive power released destroys the Jailne Lyceum, kills the entire council, and shatters the array into pieces. One piece lands on Aerlinthe, but is recovered before it can dig itself into the ground. With the defeat of the Hopeslayer, the Rhethis Eipoth (Era of Lore) is concluded by decree of Emperor Caerlin. The Torethis Eipoth (Second Era of Lore) is declared.

So Aurlanaa leaves and not long after is when the Hopeslayer shows up. This I think is what started the Clark Kent/Superman syndrome about Gromnietross having something to do with the Hopeslayer... much like when Clark Kent leaves the room Superman shows up and vice versa. Or maybe it was like birds flying south for the winter... Gromnietross fly wherever they flew for the season of darkness. Either way it is just speculation until we have more text confirming something to verify one theory or another.

The last 2 entries referring to Gromnietross may tie into something else later on... but they don't seem to have any relevance to anything we have uncovered up to this point.

TE 17: The Return of Grace. The gromnatross come down from the stars. Aurlanaa alights upon her aerie at Cunara. She looks into the eye of Kelderam, a ward of Ithaenc since her early 100's, and considered hopelessly mad. Kelderam is jauniril*, and lapses into unconsciousness. When she awakens, she weeps for a full day, and is afterwards restored in mind and spirit. In her waning years, Kelderam led the Adjanite Order, and was renowned for her wisdom and serenity. (*Translator's note: By context, "jauniril" would seem to be a verb. I have never encountered this word before, however, and know not what it means. - Bretslef)

HE 348: The Rejection. Troops returning from Valind's desecration and sack of Ithaenc climb Cunara to seek Aurlanaa's blessing. The gromnatross slays them all on sight, and departs Aerlinthe, never to be seen again.

About Shimena Keep

From the Aerlinthe Record:

RE 208: Shimena Keep constructed on the northeast islet. Shimena guarded the northern approaches to Ireth Lassel research colony from Pelagian sea-nomads.

This keep is found at 91.4n 49.7e, on the NE of the island like the entry stated. It is a large fortress infested with Diamond and Plasma Golems, Relic and Cursed Bones, and Dark Revnants. The beach surrounding the fort is guarded by Vapor Golems and pressure plates that trigger bolts of lightning that come shooting out of the statues that line the tops of the walls of the Keep. If you step on one of these pressure plates a second time, you may get a message about somethign recharging. This is just the towers that shot at you recharging for another shot. Inside, you find the three landmarks mentioned later in the Record as detailed below. Inside the Skytower, a portal to North Yanshi Advocate Tower can be found. This is one of the only 2 portals on Aerlinthe that lead off the island. The standing stone can't be selected or used and neither can the tree. You can see the locations of these landmarks in the Aerlinthe Map. Nothing else of any importance has turned up in the Keep yet.

RE 213: A larchess tree is planted within the walls of Shimena Keep.

RE 349: Memorial Stone erected in Shimena Keep, inscribed with the names of all who died in the Pelagic raids of 323.

TE 622: A Skytower arrives at Shimena, and is used to patrol the northeast waters.

 

About Lady Galaerals Murder

From the Aerlinthe Record:

TE 333: The mutilated corpse of the missionary Lady Galaeral of Ithaenc is found off the north shore.

TE 334: Imperial Inquisitor Lady Alibec arrives to investigate the murder of Galaeral. She uncovers a small group of undead hiding in the bowels of the reservoir, and destroys them. The chamber in which they performed the deed is sealed. The origin and motives of the undead remain unresolved.

TE 422: Piteous screams are heard from behind the door Inquisitor Alibec sealed in 334. It is forced open, but the room is empty, and the scene of Galaeral's murder remains as it was when the door was erected. Shrieks of pain and incomprehensible, mumbling chants are occasionally heard from the room, leading workers to whisper that the missionary's soul remains in torment at the place of her death.

The room described in these entries is located on the second floor of the Olthoi section in the Aerlinthe Reservoir Dungeon. You can see where by looking at Nodoko's maps of this section. In the room is an altar with restraints hanging off the sides, covered in blood. The walls and floor are also covered in blood as you can see here. No monsters spawn in the room. On the floor beside the altar is where you find the "Sacrificial Dagger". The 'shrieks of pain and incomprehensible, mumbling chants' can be heard in stereo while you are inside the chamber. A woman screaming, a demon talking backwards... very interesting sounds. I have provided a MP3 of these sounds to give you a better idea of the atmosphere in the room. You can hear Olthoi in the background, only because they were all at the door to the chamber trying to get in. The chamber branched off from one of the olthoi tunnels.
