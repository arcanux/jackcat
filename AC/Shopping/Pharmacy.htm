Heal Kits
If you have the Healing Skill, you can use Heal Kits to restore health. Like some of the trade skills, the heal skill has a skill check (the number at which you have a 50% chance of success). The higher your skill, the higher your chances of success. Use a better kit to add a bonus to your heal kill. The formula to determine the skill check uses your remaining health to calculate your chances. For best results, use a kit before your health gets less than 1/2 capacity.

You can purchase heal kits from vendors or pick them up as loot from many creatures. In addition, many of the Crafters on Marae Lassel will give you heal kits in exchange for various items, such as Carenzi Pelts, Siraluun Claws, and Golem Hearts. Treated Heal Kits were introduced during the Springs Sorrow event as rewards for turning in Mnemosynes. During the Dark Majesty event, they also became available as rewards for Olthoi Pincers & Claws, Vapor Golem Hearts, and Yellow Virindi Jewels. (See the Marae Lassel Crafters and Collectors page.) Plentiful Healing Kits are given as a rare reward from the Healer's Wish statue in the Bobo Quest, introduced in the Halls of the Tusker King event. The Renegade Herbal Kit is a quest reward introduced during Pillars Made of Sand.

Quest or Crafted Potions
Skilled tradespeople can also make potions using the Alchemy skill. Many quests also give potions as rewards, including the Academy Training quest which has its own special potions, and the Newbie quests. Various trophies can also be turned in to Crafters and Collectors for Potions, such as Banderling Scalps, Swamp Stones, Skeleton Skulls, Armoredillo Spines and Rat Tails. The Obsidian Enchanter in Samsur will also give you a Potion of Black Fire in exchange for an Obsidian Jewel.

Fountains
Fountains,  vats  and wells can be found scattered throughout Dereth. When you double-click on them, they cast a spell on you. The most prevalent fountain is a Revitalize Fountain which restores stamina. They can also be used with empty flasks.
