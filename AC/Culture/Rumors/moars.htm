The Moars
A note left by the sho translator, Fanzen San.
Value 15
Burden 5
Pages 3 of 3 pages full.

If you happen across this note, then the barkeeps that I contracted to supply it have done their job.

As we live in this world, we continue to unearth the secrets and history of the Empyrean. From time to time, we have come across scrolls and writings of a culture that predates both the Yalain and the Dericost.

The Falatacot have long been my area of focus although information is sparse and difficult to obtain. Recently, more and more information on them has surfaced.

This appears to correlate with the ritus that Nuhmudira enacted to weaken the Olthoi Queen.

In the time since, I have been searching for more information on the Falatacot and their experiments; but my time is limited and I have been mostly unsuccessful. I am nevertheless convinced that the Moarsmen of the Vesayen islands have some tie to the Falatacot.

If you have the time, please search the places that the Moarsmen are known to habit and bring me anything you may find. Also, come to speak with me and I can fill you in on any further details. Bring this note with you so that I will know what your business with me pertains to.

-- Fanzen San

You give Fanzen San the Translator The Moars.
Fanzen San the Translator tells you, "Ah! I see you have found my note. I asked the barkeeps on the Vesayen Islands to distribute it for me. And since you are here, giving this to me, it appears they did what I had asked."
Fanzen San the Translator tells you, "With all that has been happening of late I can't help but be intrigued by all of this Falatacot nonsense. So far we've seen a good deal about the under handed side of their people. But we know there is a lighter side to them. A less cruel side."
Fanzen San the Translator tells you, "We also know that the Moarsmen, the swamp dwelling creatures that were discovered when portals to the Vesayen Islands first opened, have some sort of connection to them. The Adjanites frequently kept them, though we are not exactly certain why."
Fanzen San the Translator tells you, "I would enjoy the opportunity to learn more about them, but I honestly have very little time outside of my translator duties to devote to researching the moarsmen."
Fanzen San the Translator tells you, "I know! Since you are here, would you mind going back to the Vesayen Islands and look for information for me? I can't leave this building, but I can read as much as I want!"
