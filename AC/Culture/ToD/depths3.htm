Live Op Announcement and Schedule
In the June Event, "From the Darkest Depths", we will be conducting a series of Live Ops which will affect the fate of each individual world of Asheron's Call. Since the Live Ops are radically different from previous Live Ops, and since there are worldwide consequences for success or failure, we are giving you some information about the structure of the Live Ops in advance so that you may prepare and plan your strategy with other members of your faction.

The Live Ops concern the conflict between the Whispering Blade and the Rossu Morta. Both the Black Knight and Count Dardante are aware of several crystals unearthed by the tumultuous awakening of Grael. Both sides are unaware of the exact dungeon in which these crystals are located, however, and are sending out scouts to find the crystal and take it for their own side.

There are some requirements to fully participate in these Live Ops. One must be a full Player Killer (not a Player Killer Lite) in order to enter the dungeon where the crystal is housed. One must also be a member of either the Rossu Morta or the Whispering Blade to return the crystal to the appropriate faction. If you are not both of these things, you will not be able to directly affect the outcome of the Live Op (although you may be able to help - see below).

For your convenience, PK altars are now present in both Chapter Houses, and will remain as permanent fixtures to more easily facilitate conflict between either group. If you want to fully participate as a PK, you may also want to ensure that you have equipped yourself appropriately for the conflict, remembering that during PK, items may be looted from your corpse by your killer. Bonded items are therefore especially valuable during PK, as is possessing sufficient high-valued items to prevent yourself from dropping your own armor and weapons in case of death. You may also wish to familiarize yourself to the myriad changes that were made to Asheron's Call in March of this year, if you have not participated in PK in a while.

If you have aversions to participating as a PK, or have not officially joined a faction, fear not - there are ways in which you may still participate in the Live Op. Any character may choose to help search the landscape, notifying your chosen faction if you happen to find a portal that has changed to only allow PKs so that the attack group from your faction can enter it to capture the crystal inside. Members of the Rossu Morta or Whispering Blade also remain with either the Black Knight or Count Dardante, in hopes that they will be able to volunteer further information that you may disseminate to your faction.
People may also express some concern as to the schedule of events. For the format we have chosen to use and the amount of manpower needed to run this Live Op, it makes the most sense to only run a single occurrence of the Live Op per world. Therefore, we are trying to schedule to accommodate the location of the majority of our player base. We believe that the 7 PM start time will allow the greatest number of players to participate in the Live Op for this one-time only event. We have rescheduled the event to occur at 8pm due to player feedback.

Finally, please be aware that, with this Live Op, we are introducing a limited amount of server divergence - depending on the performance of both factions, either the Rossu Morta or Whispering Blade will receive a small advantage on that server. Therefore, if you are or plan to be a member of either faction, it is in your best interest to participate in some manner in this Live Op. We encourage you to participate in determining the history of your world!

Hiya Maggie!
Scotty the Handy Man of LC here. You probably have and will receive more intricate names and globals, but this is the basis of this quest.

A global went up around 6ish, telling us to go to Bandit Castle. There we met an NPC who directed us to a portal 5 clicks away that portalled us to some island with a falatacot temple and tons of tendrils. On this temple were North, West, and East Dungeons, with the Everlasting Slumber port on the top.

First, we were required to enter East, which was filled with Debuff All traps at every ramp, and was, in itself, a giant maze. After taking all lefts, one could reach the bottom. I think Zyz got there first. Once more people arrived, we killed the Queen presider by the Eastern Vault door. She dropped a key, which opened the vault door and some rubble rock things (3 of them spawning every 15 seconds).

We give that to the new NPC in Bandit Castle and that flags us for the West portal. This one was pretty straight-forward. Basically, you go north at the 4 way (at drop point) and go to a ledge to a pit. This ledge also had a dispel all, so watch out. The majority of this dungeon was levers and buttons to open doors. Again, it's pretty straight-forward. Just keep pulling stuff and eventually you'll get through. On one of the last doors to open with a lever is a pressure plate in the empty room to the South. You'll know it when you're there. Going all the way through this dungeon led to another Queen presider, whom also dropped a key to the Western Vault. Inside were more rubble rock things.

After portalling back to Bandit castle and giving it to NPC once more, we go to the North. This one was all lefts, and the monsters are grouped in like 2-4's in most areas. Again, very straight forward, but LONG tunnels. At the end, guarded by the monotonous Tendrils (those things were such a pain. Remember to bring weedkiller) is the portal to the Everlasting Slumber. Inside was a jump down into a dispel all ledge overlooking a pit of acid with translucent side stepping floors like in BM3, with a big ass Soul Crystal hovering above it. Accidentally falling down into the acid, we discovered that it was all an illusion and there was an invisible floor above it. Killing the crystal was another story. It probably took about 30-45 minutes, and it kept casting "Galvanic" strikes, discs, bolts, etc. that you could not protect against. It also dispelled weapon buffs.

Upon killing that, about 50 or so shards spawned all around the pit, and bringing these shards to the guy in Bandit Castle gives xp (I'm guessing about 10% of level) and the helm. Oh yeah, the helm. Empowered Helm of Isin Dule (attached pic and stats) Zyz got the global on our server.

Thanks for updating the site. Hope you haven’t gotten your baby addicted to this yet. Wait until the age of 2 before you unleash late-night quest sessions!

-Scotty the Handy Man of LC

Whispering Blade Stomps Rossu Morta on LC!

Hiya Maggie! My name’s Scotty the Handy Man, and I participated in the live event Thursday 22nd as a Whispering Blade.

I’ve heard mixed reports from “I hate PK so I’m not going to do this crap” to “I’m PK, never been before, it’s cool” and so I’m not going to go into that.

First hand report? Well, the PKs that participated from Mad Marauders (our monarchy) all got into a fellow, as well as Xyrd (who was our non-PK fellow scout) and Jiggi-watt. (Full fellow: Lotuana, Lor’s Gal of Death, Fyacin, Xyrd, Vici, Jiggi-watt, Kuni Xao-ping, and I.)

Many met about an hour before it started to get the feel of PK or just to get to know each other. And…kill each other (The Black Knight even observed, “The people are not working together and need to be disciplined or something to that effect). When the Black Knight did show at 8, I had all my ports and portal gems ready and at hand. It took him a while to “summon the magics and gather information” but he finally said Khayyaban. So we all rushed over to Khayyaban. With GoArrow at hand, I searched the 2 closest dungeons, to find they were not PK only. Another person of my fellow did happen to find it and we all ran to the coords for Winthura's Garden and entered. Through this, people were chasing down each other and killing, no matter what side.

I found that we greatly outnumbered the Rossu Morta, and there were so few, WB vs. WB didn’t seem too out of the ordinary. Upon entering, we found the crystal in a lame spot- in the very front of the dungeon. What was even weirder was there were only like 8 people in there. Once the crystal encasement died, I happened to click at the right time and got the dropped shard. Wow! A crystal…oh yes, to the chapterhouse. I clambered for the recall orb, though no one was there to kill me. I traded with the knight and he thanked me. Then we waited another 5 minutes to get everyone together and the Knight said Baishi. So we went there, and a fellow member finally found that dungeon 2 was Trialos. Scrambling, we got there, and had a little more people there- I’d say about 15. When it died, I surprisingly got the 2nd dropped shard. Though this time, people were chasing me while I looked for my recall orb. Giving the shard to the Knight, he only thanked me again. Man of few words, man of few yards. (He was so small, many thought he was Borolean as a child.).

The third town was Stonehold, so I summoned a portal for my fellow, and we all got there (I got there conjoined to my fellow and I had to relog). Eventually, our fellow found the third dungeon, The Pit, and jumping the very bottom was that last crystal. At the very last second, one of my fellow jumped on me, blocking my view from getting that last crystal. BUT Vici, another of our fellow did get it, despite even more people. After he gave the last shard to the Knight, the Knight was like “Yay” and put the full level VII life buff crystal in the corner. Then mass interspersed killings ensued. Though I much more preferred a title, the Knight generously inscribed my Silveran Sword for me, likewise to Vici’s Silveran Wand. Adso came and threatened everyone and both Admins left, nothing more. (Full inscription: In commemoration of your skill and bravery, for retrieving two of the three crystals we sought. May your actions always bring honor to the Whispering Blade.)

Sadly, I did not die once that day. I got all bonded armor and weapons and packs-worth of DIs and didn’t die once. Oh well, I felt it challenged my own ego and wit, and was proud to have supported the Whispering Blade. I think the Rossu Morta need more people and teamwork for next time.

I’m sorry, I always forget to take pics in the most pivotal moments. I have 3 non-descript ones. How’s the baby? Did the first words pertain to Asheron’s Call? ;)

Chef Wubbie's Side of the Story!

Chef Wubbie reports: What was Chef Wubbie Thinking?

At first, I was against the idea of a PK only event; I joined in on the bashing and said I wouldn't participate. The more I thought about it and read about the event, I thought hey, why not steal the event. Myth hijacked the Shard of the Herald event and made it last much longer than it was expected to. I admit my knowledge of the recent story arc is sketchy, but it's not captivated me, and from posts I was seeing not many other people. So the 3rd Option started. I was hoping to make it clear that the players who hadn't chosen a faction should have just as much chance to affect the event as 2 small factions, and possibly gain a different outcome and have real server divergence.

I posted on the Turbine boards and told them up front I was out to get control of the event :). I also offered to give up for Pack Cows, but there was no attempt made to bribe me. There 'might' have been some controversy and speculation as to my real motives. The Devs finally posted in a thread announcing that they had considered what might happen if a 3rd party won the event, and no one working for the 3rd Option would be banned as this was a possible outcome of the event. The idea of a 3rd Option spread to other worlds. I don't know their outcomes; I hope they fared better. I would really like to see the changes made to the future of that world.

The 3rd Option was unable to gain any of the 3 crystals directly, but a 4th mercenary faction was embedded in at least one of the factions and after the event, I purchased the crystal they controlled. I was hoping that the idea of getting something different would motivate people to try the 3rd Option and see the different outcome. I had hoped that even having just 1 crystal would lead to new lore and quests to try to decipher its purpose and powers. There has been a Dev statement that its useless now and just a nice trinket to remember the event.

The upside I think for the game is how many times I had to explain the event to people and bring people up to speed that there was a 3rd Option. People showed a lot of interest, but due to time restraints, they were unable to attend. There was a lot of support and several people came forward to back my offers of MMDs to gain the crystals. I think a lot of people went from "oh a PK event" to "hey its an event where I can do something." In the end the WB won with 2 crystals, but history will have to record that there was someone from MR who got the first crystal and then offered it at auction to anyone willing to bid.
I guess a simple chef had more resources than the Count and the Black Knight. :D

There were many people who helped me and I wish I remembered all of their names, but they all know that they have a chef's help when they need it.

Attached please find the screenshot of my pretty green crystal. I hope someday its made hookable. In the meantime if you'd like to see the crystal in person, for the low low price of 5 MMDs you too can see the crystal in a trade window! :) Hey I spent 2k MMDs... I need to recoup my investment :)

Chef Wubbie - TD - I always said I was waiting for the patch where tradeskills would save the world... :D
