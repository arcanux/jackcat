New Quest - Tainted Nodes

NPC: Laedron the Geomancer: 59.9S, 88.0W

Laedron the Geomancer tells you, "Greetings! Are you here to help reclaim the land's ley lines from the corruption of T'thuum? At the behest of our noble Queen Elysa, the mage Asheron has raised six pyramids over the most tainted ley nodes in Dereth, the sites most under the grip of the wicked creature T'thuun. These pyramids must be powered! Certain creatures found in the vicinity of those nodes sometimes bear tiny crystals of concentrated, tainted mana."
Laedron the Geomancer tells you, "Bring these crystals to each of the pyramids! Once a pyramid has been sufficiently stoked by brave Derethians such as yourself, all that will be required to defeat T'thuun's power at that site is to destroy the guardian that T'thuun has stationed there... I have written a book that explains all this. I'll give you one now..."
Laedron the Geomancer gives you A Field Guide to Tainted Nodes.

A Field Guide to Tainted Nodes
A book about the nature of the threat faced by the T'thuun-tainted nodes of Dereth.

Moarsman Fort - Danby's Outpost!

Ila of HG reports: So, I went to the Moarsmen Temple by Danby's Outpost. Now it has Blighted Disgusting Moarsmen. When I killed them all, a portal opened at the bottom, and inside were a ton of Blighted Putrid Moarsmen (level 95). I was taking screenshots, so I thought, but it turns out I was using the wrong key (just recently came back to the game...).

The dungeon was pretty linear, but down near the bottom there was a fork were I spent a lot of time taking the wrong path. Go right at the bottom fork, and then carefully jump across the fading platforms (don't worry, they are still there when they are invisible).

Mages and archers will have it easier here; moarsmen sometimes collect on the other side of a jump, and if you bounce off of the you fall below and need to return to the the fork. At the end, you find a room with a human in it. He was level 120, and unfortunately I don't remember his name (thought I would have a screenshot...).

He went down easy, and dropped a book and a piece of paper. I brought them to the Captain in Tou-Tou and he gave me 11,876,313 XP (level 99) and the Title "Minister of the Sea".

I love your site, thanks for the years of dedication!"

Captain Warner tells you, "Are ye seekin' for something ta do, lad? I might know something ye could stand ta be hearin', if ya don't like these foul, tainted moarsmen no more than I do."
Captain Warner tells you, "On Ispar, I do be a ship's captain, but we do not be havin' navies here on Dereth. Instead, I be reduced to speakin' my understandin' of the lore of the sea - such as these fortresses which be appearin' upon the coast."
Captain Warner tells you, "When the blighteed moarsmen came forth, they be leavin' a way for a cunning dog such as yerself to enter their tunnels."
Captain Warner tells you, "If ya find yer way to the bottom, and ye find anything of interest, be lettin' me know what ye find. I'll be seein' what I can be doin' for ya."
You allow Captain Warner to examine your The Creation of Blighted Moarsmen.
Captain Warner tells you, "This be interestin' - I think ye should keep it. I be seein' all that I need."
Captain Warner doesn't know what to do with that.
You've earned 11,876,313 experience.
You have been awarded the title "Minister of the Sea" - congratulations!
You give Captain Warner Tattered Putrid Moarsman Ritual.
Captain Warner tells you, "Well done, ye scurvy scaliwag! Ye've done well against these scum!"

Moarsman Fort - Ayan Baqur!

Magicpipe  II of TD reports: "You can find Captin Warner in Tou Tou's Pub/Tavern

I went to ayan's moars fort put in the game a while ago as an exp quest... I killed all the moars in the area to spawn a chest with an egg like the old quest, but in addition to the chest, a portal spawned at the bottom of the building (95 levels and up)... The dungeon was full of blighted grimy moarsmen with high end stats, 800k per kill and there is and was quite a few of them... Probably not a bad place to level at 95 if you have the gear, buffs, and stats for it. Back on track, while traversing the dangers of the dungeon I came across many BM styled jump plates, which were very easy (shift forward then tap jump once) and at the end was boss human by the name of Siabot the Deranged. He dropped A lore text, and a parchment "Tattered Grimy Moarsman Ritual" (6 day timer) I took the parchment to Captain Warner in Tou Tou to receive a subpar exp reward and title."

Sweet Little Sister of HG reports:  "Inside the Ayan one it's the scary lvl 165s from outside Eastwatch. Compared to them the bloke with the weird-shaped sword was a fairly simple matter. I'm glad I had the 200+ help in that one. XP reward ... I'm not sure how it worked out those numbers. Maybe it's 10% of a level. It does NOT share in fellowship.

If nothing else, it proved Flame Chain has its uses. Some of those jumps took a while to clear."

You give Captain Warner Tattered Grimy Moarsman Ritual.
Captain Warner tells you, "Well done, ye scurvy sacliwag! Ye've done well against these scum!"
You've earned 56,184,660 experience.
You have been awarded the title "Champion of the Sea" - congratulations!
