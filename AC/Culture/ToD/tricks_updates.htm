PvP Improvements - 11/11/05 (by Frelorn)

We know that the PvP community has been feeling a bit left out as of late, and we want to rectify this as soon as we can. Anything we add or change that involves PvP requires a lot of testing to make sure that it can be balanced. That being said, we wanted to come here and get your feedback on a couple of the ideas that we had mind. Please understand that we have not tested these ideas nor have we ironed out all the technical barriers to implementation. But before we go ahead and attempt something like this we want you, the players to let us know what you think. Now, I know what you will say… What about the ideas we have already given to you? We still would like to implement some of these ideas, but a lot of them require a great amount of tech time and testing, and we do not want to rush into anything that could make things worse instead of better.

The first idea we have discussed is a way for players to add a moderate bonus to any pre-existing Slayer effect on whatever weapon they are wielding at the time. There of course would be limitations to this so that it could not be exploited or abused.

The second idea was along the same lines as the one listed above, except this would be a way to add a moderate bonus to the attack mod of the weapon you are wielding. The same rules would apply, meaning there would be some limitations to this so that the ability would not be abused. Also note that the bonus to the attack mod would be on top of any tinkering that may have been done to the weapon.

What we want from you the players is feedback on what you think about these two ideas. I will again say that we have not even tested this yet and we are not sure that the current tech will allow us to do these things. We felt it was important to get your feedback on these things before we went forward with attempting any of these ideas.

Re: Respec Timer Redesign- 11/14/05 (by Lupus Argenteus )

Well, we asked for feedback, and we certainly received it, with opinions ranging all over the spectrum. To an extent, this was expected. To address a few points:

1.Why don't you use a 1 day/no timer?

We would like template selections to mean something. We encourage you to make new characters when you want to try a new template, rather than respeccing each time. The ability to retrain is there, but we do not wish to completely trivialize it.

2.Why don't you leave the timers at 2 weeks?

There is a significant part of the population who has been asking for some change. We are attempting to implement something which will allow templates broken by past games systems changes to be mended, while still assigning a level of importance to early choices.

3.Why don't you implement a cool-down timer?

We believe that currently players are familiar enough with the current game mechanics to be able to decide what sort of character they wish to play. In the past, when we have made major changes to games systems (such as the implementation of the Salvaging skill) we have given characters free untrains of the skills affected - this is something we will continue to consider when we make major gameplay changes. Between those two points, we believe that there is no need for a cool-down timer on retrains.

4.Why does it affect other players what I play?

We must balance the gameplay of Asheron's Call against the entire community of characters. In this sense, yes, what one player's choices are for their character affects the game and the players around them.

We have taken several of the comments into consideration. Here is the revised retrain proposal:

Untrain
1st-4th gems: Instant
5th-8th gems: 1 week timer
9th-16th gems: 2 week timer
17th and above: 3 week timer

Specialization
1st-2nd gems: Instant
3rd-4th gems: 1 week timer
5th-8th gems: 2 week timer
9th and above: 3 week timer

Attribute
1st-18th: Instant
19th-36th gems: 1 week timer
37th and above: 2 week timer

We will not place a 3 month quest timer on turning in a Tessera after all. However, there will still be a 2 week hand-in timer on Tesserae.
