Feb. 23, 2005
Legions Press Preview Event

On the morning of Wednesday, February16th, I set out for the airport. It was time to show the press what our AC1 and AC2 expansion packs were all about. The event was to be held in San Francisco, at Shanghai 1930. Personally, I like flying, but the trek to San Francisco is no small journey; it’s almost literally coast to coast. I’m not one of those people who can sleep on a plane, either. Oh well, I was sure there would be a decent movie to help pass the time, and I wanted plenty of time to review the event schedule. It had been a pretty busy couple of days on the forums, with the AC2HQ exclusive screenshot series and the March content plans we’d posted only just the evening before.

I was soon on my way. It was to be a couple hours to Chicago, a short layover, then the larger half of the trip from there to San Francisco. Once on the San-Fran flight, I learned we would have FOUR movies to choose from! Sweeeeet! Alas, however, they were all bad. It was a blessing when the pick of the litter, Catwoman, glitched out. I was left to surf the alternate channels. Luckily, they had Discovery, Food Network, and HGTV. Wow, just like being at home! I learned that polar bears are cannibals, and can smell a dead seal from several miles away, and that Pinot Noir is one of the more versatile wines for pairing with foods of any kind (not all in the same show, of course).

We touched down around 5:30 PM, went through the typical “look like a mutant as you try to pop your ears” exercise, located the cabstand, and soon arrived at the hotel. At the check-in counter, I ran into Jessica, which was wonderful, because it meant that I was at the right hotel, at approximately the right time.

I had just enough time to stow my stuff before it was time to convene for the team dinner, at a little German restaurant called Suppenkuche. We’d been griefing our marketing guy earlier since he had set up the dinner plans, warning him that we had refined tastes and were accustomed to only the finest fare. He assured us that Suppenkuche was German for “soup kitchen”, but I think soup would have been disqualified from the menu for not being dense enough. The food was very authentic; fried potato, pureed potato, boiled potato…like a truckload of rocks being dumped down a well, but it was all sooo goooood! And German beer, of course, most excellent. It was great to see Ibn and Jessica again, and we talked of many things, but we made it a relatively early night. Everyone’s thoughts were on the big day tomorrow, and we wanted to be in top form.

Thursday, February 17th I woke before the alarm went off (I so HATE that I do that!). As I got ready, I caught some great early-season Buffy (the Zander hyena episode, classic). Then I headed down to the breakfast bar, where Citan and Argus were already noshing. The coffee was excellent. The rest of our East and West-coast staff soon joined us, and we all made our way to the nearby Shanghai.

My first impression of the location – beautiful. Shanghai is a members-club sort of establishment, with special speakeasy-style cigar rooms, a jazz bar, and lush decor. There was an alcove next to our space containing a small number of exclusive lockers for members to store their primo cigars. One of these lockers belongs to the Governor…a.k.a. Ahhhnold. Our event would take place in a well-appointed sitting room with its own bar, and a second side room for our top-secret meetings and…boxes. Both rooms were furnished with the most comfortable leather chairs you can imagine. I recalled the image from Memorex ads I think, of the guy sitting waaay back in his chair getting blown away by his awesome audio tape recordings (which is pretty funny all on its own). Well, that was one of THESE chairs. Oh, and the coffee they brought out was even better than the last 2 cups I’d had at breakfast. I did kind of get pretty wired on coffee in a very short amount of time, but hey, we were excited! This was to be our first press event as a publisher, and wired was a fine place to be, because there was much to be done.

We spent the morning setting up the space, and the tech equipment needed for our various demo stations and promo movies (you’ll be seeing a web version of the Legions promo movie from this event soon). The swag kits and name badges were assembled, while the technical crew decked the place with flat-panels. Our Sony partner crew arrived at this point, along with our publicity partners.
 


Covert SWAG Ops in the secret headquarters


Our main Legions demo station was for four – one leader and three players, who would be guided through a simple Knorr quest to get a feel for the game. The quest started on a farm in a really nice rolling hills location, with some vibrant fall-colored trees you haven’t seen just yet. Our users would then teleport to Vothardun, the creepy foggy locale the team affectionately refers to as “Zombie Town” (won’t you take me to…) to complete the quest. The characters were pre-rolled with a good set of flashy hotkey skills and an inability to actually die. There was an Empyrean Mentalist, a Lugian and a Tumerok decked out in new armor and weapons even I had never seen before, and…our secret surprise.

One of the things we were most proud of and excited to announce at this event was our pre-order bonus. There he stood, next to his fellow adventurers, ready to take his place amongst the free peoples of Dereth – the playable drudge. For those of you wondering why we haven’t announced the winners for the Skill-naming contest yet, well, it was because the winning skill name belonged to a Drudge skill! The Drudge exhibits the sense of humor that the AC franchise is known for in all it’s lovingly crafted glory. Wait till you see. Anyway, players who pre-order Legions will be able to create and play drudges right away. At a later date, a quest to unlock the race will be available, but this quest will have a pretty high level-requirement (60-ish). Personally, I plan to take the easy route and pre-order the bonus bundle - when it is available, which is not right now, but soon. You’ll be seeing more about the Drudge in the coming month.
 


Setting up the Legions 4-player demo station

So where was I? Oh yes, so we have the 4-player guided demo station, and a second private station for additional showings. AC1 had a demo station where they could show off the before and after of the graphics engine upgrade that will ship with Throne of Destiny. They’ve done a great job, by the way, and they have…penguins…which I think speaks for itself.

Setup was completed in plenty of time, and then it was just a matter of going over Argus and Jessica’s talks about the expansion packs, and last-minute walkthrough reminders from marketing. At one point, a regular member of a gentlemen’s club at Shanghai accidentally wandered into our area, and peeling him away from the Legions demo station was no easy task. Marketing was happy to take his business card, but he’ll just have to pre-order like the rest of us!
 


Morning setup: One of the Preview Movie stations, flanked by Throne of Destiny and Legions Posters


I was to begin the evening at reception, meeting and greeting the press, and getting them their nametags. Two ladies from our partner teams were also on hand for reception, and we earned the nickname “Charlie’s Angels” later on when things got rough, but I’ll get to that. The reception area happened to be right where those special cigar lockers I mentioned earlier were. Our event was to the right, and the rest of the club was to the left. Around 5:45, the press began to arrive. One of our first guests was Tapuaua from Warcry, and she came bearing coffee and chocolate! I had a Reece’s peanut butter cup moment then, and resolved to experiment with the “Lik’m Aid” method of combining chocolate bars and ground coffee in the near future. An idea that’s time has come, I think.

Well, by now apparently our wandering guest from earlier had told several of his club friends that the other half of the club was being used and was therefore off-limits, but there were games in there. Reception became a rather interesting place to be when unauthorized individuals suddenly started trying to get into the press event for a peek. We heard several likely stories, ranging from “I’m just going in there to use the restroom”, “I’m just going to the bar real quick” and “It’s ok, I’m a member of this establishment” to the last-ditch “I’m a waiter here” (yes, he really did say that, and no, he wasn’t a waiter). We saw through all these clever ploys, and refused to be swayed by the pointing out of their own cigar lockers on the cigar wall (“I have a box here, you know, and over there, that’s Ahhnold’s box). They too, will have to wait for the expansion (except for Ahhnold, I think we could sneak him into the Playtest).

The event was a great success, and the members of the media reacted very favorably to Legions, noting how graphically excellent the game looks. They also got quite a kick from our Drudge, with his faithful “rabid bunny” fighting at his side. At 9 PM our event concluded, and we bid a fond farewell to the last of our press guests. The AC2 community has asked that they be given a heads-up when Legions coverage is coming. Well…heads-up! You’ll be seeing reports from the event starting as soon as this week, and continuing through launch.

When all had departed, we gathered for a moment of warm basking, and the club brought us cigars and some pretty incredible Tequila - so good it actually demanded sipping, Patron Añejo, I believe it was called. As Ferris Bueller once said, “If you have the means, I highly recommend picking one up”. We had a toast, and afterwards, breakdown commenced. Everything was pretty much completed by 11 PM, but spirits were high and the night was young. Suffice it to say that Ibn and I made good and frequent use of the threat, “That’s going in the blog!” for the rest of the evening.

In the morning, I made my way to the airport for another day of flying. At least, I thought, I would surely have a better choice of movie than the vile “not-Michelle Pfeiffer” Catwoman. As the flight got underway, however, I discovered that this time, there was only one movie…Shall We Dance. No, no…I think we shall not. Let me tell you guys, mainstream America can be a very scary place. Thank goodness I had a blog that needed writing.
