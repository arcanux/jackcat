From the Turbine Site - Feb. 24, 2004 - Ibn's Write-up

On Thursday February 17, Turbine held its first-ever press event as an independent publisher, providing demonstrations of Asheron's Call: Throne of Destiny and Asheron's Call 2: Legions to representatives from both print and online media. This is Ibn's running account of the day, pieced together from notes taken on his PDA.

8:15AM: Up and out of bed. The hotel we're staying at, Hotel Griffin, is literally next door to the location of the event, Shanghai 1930, so there's no travel time needed to get there. Jessica and I had flown in yesterday and met up with the rest of the Turbine team at the hotel. Argus, Citan and Ramen were there from the AC2: Legions team along with Jason (Director of Corporate Communications) and Jim (Director of Marketing). Over dinner we discussed the plan for the day, and I made sure to get to bed relatively early. Flying takes a lot out of me and I knew I'd need to be sharp today.

8:45AM: We meet in the hotel restaurant for our complimentary continental breakfast, as well as some much-needed coffee for the folks who drink it. The weather is pretty miserable – overcast and drizzly, with heavier rain expected later in the evening.

8:54AM: Jason asked us to be at Shanghai by 9, so we head over. Our reserved space is downstairs, in a part of the restaurant that's used as a private club. The best way to describe the space is one word: plush. The chairs are deep and soft and hard to get out of, and everything is purple cloth or dark wood. The place is also a cigar club – on the way in, someone points out that part of the collection is reserved for Governor Schwarzenegger. We figure out what parts of the space will be reserved for AC's demo and likewise for AC2's, so we start unpacking boxes.

9:15AM: Small problem – the servers aren't here! The plan was to have two PCs – one would have the February client and server running on it, and the second would have the latest Throne of Destiny build running so that the press could see the marked graphical improvement. I'd configured two machines back in Santa Monica, and Ski-Dawg and I had packed them up and shipped them out on Monday. We'd even received confirmation on Tuesday that they'd arrived safely! Where were they?

I always like to prepare for the worst, so I'd also installed both servers and clients on a company laptop that I'd brought with me. In a pinch we could run the demo off of that, but it'd be a lot better to have two machines.

I call Ski-Dawg back in Santa Monica who checks the tracking info and starts calling FedEx. This is when we discover that no one's cell phones work in the basement where we're setting up.

9:30AM: Servers found! As they'd arrived a day earlier than the shipment from Westwood, our host at Shanghai had put them into a different room. Whew! Jason needs me to wait for our audiovisual vendor to arrive – we've contracted a local company to provide rack-mounted machines to run the AC2 client, about a dozen standard flat-screen monitors and three wide-screen plasma screens for the Throne of Destiny cinematic, the Throne of Destiny demo and the Legions gameplay video. So I go upstairs to wait for them to arrive with our equipment.

9:45AM: Citan comes upstairs to relieve me – he's done setting up the AC2 server, so he waits for the AV vendor while I go downstairs to set up the AC and AC:TD boxes.

10:15AM: We've blown a fuse and the AV vendor hasn't arrived yet, so I'm sitting on the floor, in the dark, using our one monitor to set up the machines. The February box comes up and tests fine, so I get to the task of installing a newer build on the AC:TD box.

We're at the point in development now where just about all the back-end work is done so we're getting more client functionality every single day. For example, between the Monday build and the Wednesday build, we got the in-game radar, creature examination and drag-and-drop hooked up. So Wednesday, right before we left, I burned the latest client and server to CD and took them with me on the plane.

Now I'm unzipping these files and installing them so that our guests will see the latest and greatest!

Two women from the PR firm that we hired are also here to help out. While Citan and I have been testing equipment they and the other Turbine folk have been packing gift bags for our guests. (I just realized – I'm not even sure what was in those bags because I never got one for myself! Better remember to harass Jason about that...)

11:00AM: Much improved! We have lights, the AV vendor is here and is lugging equipment down to our space, and the demo boxes have the right software and are running fine!

11:15AM: All of our equipment is good to go but it'll take a while yet for the AV vendor to set up all of their stuff, so we take a break for lunch. Our hosts provide the Chinese food. It's EXCELLENT.

12:20PM: The vendors are hooking up the 8 AC2 client machines, but I grab their attention long enough to get the monitors, plasma screen and video splitter that I need to get the AC:TD demo set up. We've switched around locations so that the AC2 playable demo has enough space. The February screen will be sitting on the bar, I'll be sitting behind it at the controls of the AC:TD client. Video for the AC:TD box is split between my screen and the plasma where Jess will be talking to the guests. You can see a good picture of the setup on AC Vault.

2:20PM: OK! All the hardware is hooked up, we only blew one more fuse, and we've done trial runs through both presentations. Right now folks are playing the AC2 demo. Three people from our distribution partner, Station Publishing, have arrived and will be helping us with the event. Everything appears to be going pretty smoothly!

3:00PM: Back to my room at the hotel for a break, also to shower and shave and put on my "nice" clothes. I didn't want to be under a table hooking up PC cables while wearing my dress shirt!

4:00PM: We gather in the Shanghai again. The 8 AC2 clients have blown another fuse, so the AC2 folks have decided to cut it down from 8 playable machines to 4 playable and 1 demo machine.

Jess and I do the full presentation for the rest of the team, who ask questions that they think are likely to be asked by the guests.

The presentation itself is pretty straightforward. We start off with the February client standing just outside Glenden Wood, and Jess explains that when we bought the franchise from Microsoft the first thing we wanted to do with AC was give it a makeover. She explains how we upgraded every single texture in the game and completely rewrote the client graphic engine, and then says, "And here's how the game will look when we launch on May 11." That's when she turns on the plasma screen showing the AC:TD client in the exact same location, and everyone goes, "Oooooh! Ahhhh!"

While Jess talks about all of the improvements, I run the character around Glenden Wood, showing off the improved landscape textures, building textures, and skybox. Next, we teleport to Sawato, where you can see the gorgeous new swamp trees and Sho building textures.

The next location is Sanamar, Viamontian capital on Dereth. We walk up and say hello to the Viamontian town crier, noting his blue skin, and then we run up into Sanamar proper. We pass through the gates and run through the town, as the Great Hall looms on the hill to our right. The statue of Varicci II casts its shadow down on us as Jess talks about the Viamontians as both a playable heritage group and the main adversary of the expansion pack.

Then we portal to a frozen forest near the Shadow Pass on Vanguard Isle, to show off some of the new creatures. A Penguin slides up to me and starts swinging, but can't touch my admin character. Jess mentions that there are bigger Penguins, and I spawn an Uber Penguin. These are the Penguins that look like someone's spent a million dollars upgrading them. They chain-cast. It hurts.

I spend a few minutes running around the forest, being beaten down by Viamontian Knights and their fast two-handed weapons, being blasted by Maddened Fiun spells and being gnawed on by Eaters. Jess mentions that we've got new equipment to take on these creatures – rares. So I smite or delete all the creatures around me and show off some of the absolutely gorgeous rares that are coming in the expansion pack. Shields like the Twin Ward and the Dread Marauder. New armor like the Patriarch Coat (which is what happens when you ask an artist for a new "coat of armor"). New weapons like the gigantic sword named Morrigan's Vanity, the spiked mace called the Star of Tukal, and my personal favorite: a vicious-looking axe named Count Renari's Equalizer. Whoever finds one of these is going to be very happy.

Then we take questions, and we're done.

The plan is that we'll wine and dine our guests for a while, and then we'll do the AC1 presentation followed by the AC2 presentation and playable demo. Afterwards we'll be free to answer any more questions and show off any other features of AC:TD that we want to show off. Our guests should start arriving between 6 and 6:30.

6:38PM: It's 8 minutes after "arrival time" and many of our guests haven't arrived yet. We get word that the pouring rain has caused a number of accidents on the freeways, making San Francisco's already-formidable rush hour traffic even worse. Jason decided to tweak the plan on the fly – we'll do demos for smaller groups, more frequently, so that the earlier arrivals aren't waiting for the others. Jess and I do our first demo for an editor from GameSpot. Two fansite reps have also arrived: Kristopher from AC Vault and Tapuaua from AC2 Warcry.

8:32PM: Jess and I have run the demo about a half-dozen times. We've got it down to a science – when she's saying, "Now let's show you Sanamar," I've already got the command to teleport there typed out and ready to go. I'm thrilled at the response from our guests – jaded gaming editors are looking at the upgraded client and saying, "Wow." I know I ate some appetizers at some point, but it's all a blur of answering questions, running the demo, and stopping every time I walk past the TV showing the AC:TD opening cinematic. It's just gorgeous. If you've downloaded the video that we provided on CD to our guests, think about this – that's only the first half of the video! Even though I know the voice-over by heart I just can't stop watching it or listening to it. "The Duke and his daughter..."

(Trivia: I auditioned for the voice-over. I didn't get the part. Ah the challenges of trying to break into Hollywood! J/K!)

9:16PM: Almost all the guests have left. I'm playing the AC2 demo and manage to get my all-but-unkillable demo character struck dead instantly. Citan laughs at me. I think it's time to start breaking down and packing the equipment.

10:00PM: All of our equipment is packed and ready for shipping back to the Westwood and Santa Monica studios. We're all exhausted but happy – the event went really well, everyone seemed to have a good time, and both games should get some great press coverage as our marketing campaigns roll forward. Time to bust out the cigars!

We stayed at the Shanghai for another hour or so, and then a bunch of us hit the town. As much as I'd love to publish that part of the record of the day, I don't think any of us want that much blackmail material posted on the web. Ha! Luckily for me Jessica tolerated my less-than-coherent state when she and I left for the airport at 6AM the next morning.

In conclusion, I'd say the event was a success, especially for Turbine's first independent outing. It was a lot of work, both on the day of the event and the weeks leading up to it, but it was also a lot of fun. The more press we get for these games, the more characters we'll see in the world when we ship. This is a good thing, for us and for you! Here's to the rest of the countdown to launch going as well as the Press Event!
