Ned Cleversun reports on the Virindi Executor
Ok. Preliminary report, Virindi Executor. They have resisted all my attempts to Imperil or vuln them. I do have 5% vitae, however.

When I attack them with my bow, which has BD 6 and other buffs, I get 2 point hits. For comparison, I hit Umbris Shadows, unvulned, for 35-40 points. They evade me a LOT, about 10-15 times (not percent) more than the Umbris. The good news is that I didn't take too much damage from them, or haven't so far. One other point: they seem to level very easily. The one I fought leveled in the midst of our battle, and he didn't put that much damage on me.

Virindis Attack Ayan Baqur
If you wandered into Ayan on the morning of April 1, 2001, you might have thought Turbine was playing an elaborate April Fool's joke on you. Virindi now stand in place of all the shopkeepers! What in the world???

The evening of March 31, the Virindi attacked! The shopkeepers high-tailed it out of town and hid deep in the Panopticon Dungeon (59.1S, 84.6W). This dungeon is restricted to levels 32+ and it may be tied to! Looks like getting to Ayan will no longer be the adventure it used to be. 

Inside the dungeon adventurers found a unique Virindi, Adirred the Theoretician, who cried the following message as he was killed:

"The Virindi's cloak almost implodes inward as it shrieks in your mind, "But the experiment was going so well! We were so close to breaking past the Singularity's structuralism..."

As someone mentioned on the boards, Adirred is Derrida spelled backwards. Jacques Derrida is a famous literary theorist and a leading deconstructionist. All but the most stubborn scholars find his writing almost impenetrable. Plus he's French!! ;-)

For more complete information on the night's event, read Crossroads of Dereth's write-up on the Virindi takeover!

You can also read the new Virindi Ulgrim rumors here. The charming fellow now has a taste for Red Wine.
