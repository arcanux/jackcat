Hiyas Maggie - I love your site and appreciate all the hard work that you must put into it. :)

Well...........last night the servers came up earlier than planned and I heard a rumor about fishing. I ran to my favorite water spot and found a hut there with a Tackle Master in it. He can craft fishing poles out of Wood Golem Hearts or Golem Jo's. Joy upon joy I had one on me. :) To fish you need a pole and a one day fishing license. The fishing license is sold at the same hut for a C note.

After fishing about fifteen minutes I had about 30 minnows, 4 guppies, several rusted weapons, and a couple pieces of cheap jewelry. My fishing skill was 4 and the other fishermen and women thought that your skill was based on how many guppies you caught. It seems true in my case and all of their cases so far.

There are a few bugs.............such as hooking other fishermen and women (roleplay only), and mispelling of some things (ie you throw your fishing hole in the water....... ). I think it should be pole. I broke my line once..............but it seemed to fix itself in just a few seconds. I love these new fishing poles. :) Someone said they were going to take their fish to be mounted....................and that they put a minnow up on a hook in their house. I don't know if the crafters can mount fish, or if the minnows, etc are house hookable or not.

So...................to quit rambling so that others can come and fish too, I will give out the secret directions to my fishing spot and the Tackle Master.

Items Needed Wood Golem Heart or Golem Jo, C Note that can be bought from the Mage in town, Patience...........lots of patience.

A modified version of Hea Karenua's casting buadren. This is a fine old drum from the Tumerok world, rich with fetishes and power. The bones of centuries-dead drudges, mosswarts, and banderling, rattle against one another in the wind. The skin stretched across it, legend tells, is made from flesh of Zhuaruf, a Tumerok birthed from the union of the Hea shaman Niheaua and his own dream-spun daughter. There is an inscription around the rim which reads, "To release one's brother from the clinging threads of a hateful dream."
