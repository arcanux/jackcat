Rumors

In Yanshi, the deaf oracle has broken his lifelong silence, chanting a litany of baleful portent: the world-devouring serpent uncoils from the cosmic abyss. Bael'Zharon passes like a shadow before the sun, conjuring his altars of strife within the very towns of Dereth, and those who patiently count the stars mark a score fewer each night. Pass the next four days in the manner that appears best to you. The world is in the making, and the agents of chaos sanctioned to destroy.

From the "Annals of Wardiel", found on the body of the chronicler Wulfdan,

"Fools," muttered Wardiel as he inspected his battle-axe's edge for the umpteenth time, noting the droplet of blood now oozing from his thumb with grim satisfaction.

"They dare shun me from the lands of Dereth. Well, they'll pay for such impudence with their lives."

In one cat-like move he leapt to his feet, swinging the axe above his head as he let loose a malevolent battle cry - his eyes growing wider and redder as the crackling electricity emanating from the cold-steel blade slowly sheathed his body. His fury momentarily spent, he again sat down, the air now heavy with ozone and the sickly sweet smell of burning hair.

"They'll get no quarter from me. None! These dimwitted sots will find my axe at their necks soon enough, and I'll send their souls to feed Bael'Zharon."

Wardiel's cruel features lightened for a moment on this last thought, as he pondered the population of Dereth under his iron-shod heel.

"Aye, Dereth is more filled with harlequins than heroes these days. It sits as ripe as a melon on harvest day, waiting only for me to bring my blade to its delicate skin and spill its treasures onto my plate."

Wardiel again looked out from his high prospect over the lands of Dereth. "Yes, it is time," he grimaced. "And I am so very hungry."

Developer's Chat - August 6 , 1999

+MrQuazarr@Zone> Welcome everyone. We will begin the conversation with the designers and program manager of Asheron's Call in 20 minutes!
+MrQuazarr@Zone> Welcome everyone and thank you for attending the Asheron's Call status update from Gen Con. We have three very special guest with us today to give an update on the status of Asheron's Call,
+MrQuazarr@Zone> a Massive Multiplayer Online Role-playing Game developed by Turbine Entertainment and produced by Microsoft.
+MrQuazarr@Zone> We have Chris Pierson, AC_GenCon, who is a game designer at Turbine, and working on Asheron's Call.
+MrQuazarr@Zone> Also joining us is Toby Ragaini, auberon@turbine, who is the design director at Turbine, and the lead designer on Asheron's Call.
+MrQuazarr@Zone> And finally the Program Manager for Asheron's Call, Matthew Ford, stucco@zone.
+MrQuazarr@Zone> Each will give a brief update on the status of Asheron's Call and talk about the new and exciting features and updates in the recent Beta 2 release.
+MrQuazarr@Zone> Following their presentation, you will be able to ask questions of each. Not all questions may be able to be answered as the game is still in development.
+MrQuazarr@Zone> If you have questions about you potential status in the beta or access to the beta, these three will not be able to help you there unfortunately. You will need to write to ashtest@microsoft.com.
+MrQuazarr@Zone> With that said, let me first introduce Chris Pierson who is live at Gen Con right now.

AC_GenCon> Hi. We're on our second day now, and the booth is pretty much packed with people. They seem pretty impressed with the game.
AC_GenCon> We were mentioned in the local paper, which seems to have made people give us an extra-careful look.

+MrQuazarr@Zone> Thanks Chris! Next Toby Ragaini. Toby, what is now on your front of Asheron's Call?

auberon@Turbine> hi everyone
auberon@Turbine> we've been very busy here and beta 2 seems to be shaping up nicely
auberon@Turbine> we've already got allegiances consisting of several hundred players, and many people are playing over 6 hours a day!
auberon@Turbine> overall i'm very excited about the way the game is shaping up, and i hope i can answer your questions tonight

+MrQuazarr@Zone> Thanks Toby! Finally, Matthew Ford, the program manager for Asheron's Call, tell us in your eyes about Asheron's Call.

Stucco@Zone> Thanks... hi everyone!
Stucco@Zone> We are now entering a major new pahse of beta.
Stucco@Zone> We have let all our existing beta players in on the "beta 2" code and content
Stucco@Zone> It's awesome. People are loving it. The game looks and plays better than ever, which is saying a lot.
Stucco@Zone> We now have about 300 online at once, BUT...
Stucco@Zone> I'm pleased to make a big announcement!
Stucco@Zone> We have just sent out email to thousands more of the beta testers who are on the waiting list
Stucco@Zone> They will soon be getting all the info they need to install and play the game.
Stucco@Zone> We will be aggressively letting in more people all the time... if you are on the official beta list (and you know who you are) keep an eye on the inbox for the next couple weeks!
Stucco@Zone> We are looking forward to seeing the game flourish with new blood... so to speak.. heh heh.

+MrQuazarr@Zone> Thank you all three! Sounds like Asheron's Call is really shaping up to be one of, if not the top, Massive Multiplayer Online Role-playing Games.

+MrQuazarr@Zone> To ask a question, go to the CHAT menu and select "Ask Question". You will be moved up to the Waiting Queue. You will be advanced to the Asking Queue when your turn to ask a question comes up.
+MrQuazarr@Zone> When you are done with your question please type "DONE".

+MrQuazarr@Zone> Each person will be allowed one question. Due to the constraints of Gen Con, we will have to stop the questions promptly at 4:30 PDT.

Evaksrathi> Q: What if any sort of new innovations does AC have to offer to gamers over other games of the same genre? And how do you plan to incorperate the advancement of technology, will AC be up-to-speed when 300-600mhz cpus and 16-32meg 3d cards become the...
Evaksrathi> "norm" in the coupleyears to come? It seems that computer technology is getting better, faster, cheaper at quite an astounding pace. (Done)

Stucco@Zone> AC has an amazing detail on the fly system...
Stucco@Zone> It detects your frame rate...
Stucco@Zone> And as your rate gets better it pours more polys onto the screen
Stucco@Zone> So if you have an older hardware it's very playable...
Stucco@Zone> But if you have a screamer of a machine it scales up nicely

auberon@Turbine> thats true, also
auberon@Turbine> we can update the artwork with newer higher polygon model to effectively keep up with the improvements in tech
auberon@Turbine> i'd also like to address the feature comparison question
auberon@Turbine> AC was designed to support thousands of gamers in some interesting ways
auberon@Turbine> one of these is the emphasis on social systems
auberon@Turbine> some of you might be familiar with the allegiance, which allows character to recruit other players, and gain experience and rank for doing this
auberon@Turbine> in beta, we are seeing some very cool player interaction which is derived from people roleplaying in their allegiances

+MrQuazarr@Zone> Oewyn000, you're next

AC_GenCon> Also, spells will consume components (as will spell research).

Stucco@Zone> as for houses...
Stucco@Zone> Right now Dereth is a frontier world, everyone has just arrived.
Stucco@Zone> It's a bunch of small towns in a wilderness...
Stucco@Zone> And the lifestyle is nomadic...
Stucco@Zone> We have the capability of letting people buy or quest for houses...
Stucco@Zone> But frankly we are planning for the long term here...
Stucco@Zone> And we want to avoid "gentrification" at the moment...
Stucco@Zone> don't worry, the world will evolve...
Stucco@Zone> And you'll see Dereth grow into cities...
Stucco@Zone> But for now we want to keep people wandering the wilderness, finding their home.
