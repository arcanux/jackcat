Halls of the Helm (18.7N, 1.6E) near Zaikhal
Recommended Levels: 10-20

Creatures: Zombies, Undead, Liches and Guardian of the Helm (Unique Undead)

A great place to level your lowbies. Should be simple with enough endurance and basic life protects. Highly recommended for archers as undead have horrendous melee defence.

North Glendon Prison (also called Glendon Woods Prison) 31.8N, 25.5E
Recommended Levels: 15-25

Creatures: Zombies,Undead, Liches

I've tried hunting at the Lugian Citadel, and pretty much anywhere else.  The easiest and quickest experience I've gotten is off of anything already dead. :) Zombies and Undead are great experience... about 950 for zombies, and undead aren't that far off.  They don't heal, they can't dodge, and while they do cast spells quite frequently, they're still easy to handle.  I can kill a Zombie in about 10 seconds, and in areas like the GW Prison and Halls of Helm, you can go through an almost endless horde of Zombies.  Unfortunately, if someone else is around you'll find the Zombies just don't respawn fast enough to keep you leveling steadily. Great for the un-tweaked characters... extremely easy for low-levels with GSA and Atlan Weapons.

Zombie Castle (45.1S, 39.2E)
Recommended Levels: 12-20

Creatures: Zombies (about 10-12).  Surrounding area has Reedsharks, Auroch Cows and Bulls.

Great place to work on your Magic Defense.  Difficult for solo mage because Zombies love to drain mana, but melees can get really good xp.  Lots of magic clothing gets dropped.  Very private.  All the Zombies are in and around a single tower.  Spawn rate is good enough that individuals and low level groups won't clear the tower before spawn starts again.

Walled Portals (21.6S, 74.5W)
Recommended Levels: 15+

Creatures: Drudges Lurkers, Stalkers, and Raveners, sometimes Tusker Slaves, and every now and then a random baddie, such as Virdini, Shadows, Gold Wasps, etc.

You can get to it by giving the Drunken Madman (53.0S, 61.0E) near Baishi some sake. This place is great to drain from. You're in a walled-off portion of land while all the baddies are outside and can't get to you. Every now and again a drudge jumps the wall, but you can jump in the Ridge Tower portal if needed.

Mage Tower (31.6S, 37.2E)
Recommended Levels: 8-20

Creatures: Shreth, Wasps, Reedsharks to Elders, Limestone Golems, Dillos, Skeletons and Auroch to Bulls.

Comments: Good for Archers and Mages. There is a lifestone right next to the tower. Mage inside sells minimal goods. Closest town is Uziz and it is a long run, but the tower is usually empty so you get all the kills. Spawn rate is set so you can clear 5 spawns from the tower and still have time to loot. Bring lots of comps or arrows if you don't have ls recall and a town portal tied. Hardest part is getting there to use the ls. Bring a higher level friend to help clear the way.

Tibri's Cave (24.2N, 43.2E)
Recommended Levels: 10-20

Creatures: Banders!  Up to a Captain and a few Black Rats.

Nice place to power level on Banders.  They can come in big packs with a fairly fast respawn.  Lifestone nearby, as well as a nice portal to the surface in case things get to hot for you.  Not far from Fort Witshire, so you can usually try out both places and pick up a quest item (Tibris Fire Spear).  Also, nice hunting outside as well.  Kill the Banders near the entrance of the cave and many more will come running from the nearby countryside.  The cave makes a nice haven as well before you jump into the dungeon.

Undecided of LC
Level 47 Sword

Beach North of Arwic (42.0N, 63.0W)
Recommended Levels: 10-15 (extreme), 12-17 others

Creatures: Shore Armoredillos

From Arwic, run east/northeast to the beach.  Run north once you hit the beach.  You will eventually come to some standing stones near the beach with Shallows Slayers around them.  Keep heading north to about 42N, 63W.  You should see some stones in the water close to this location.  This is where the Shore Dillos should spawn.

Be careful not to let them get behind you and wear blade protection if you have it.  If possible, take some throwing weapons along to lure them out one at a time, as they are not sociable critters and don't help each other out.

If I remember correctly, they give about 1.2K XP per dillo.  That is not bad for a level 10-12 character.

I usually hunt these until I can head to citadel at level 15-17.  It is quite a run, so keep in mind that you will want to stock up on healing kits etc. if you don't want to run back to town often to restock. It works great to have a buddy of higher level drop off supplies for you if possible.

Keep in mind that this location is not for the timid.

Smuggler's Cove (29.5N, 91.8E) across the river from Holtburg
Recommended Levels: 8-15

Creatures: Banderlings, including a Captain.

Hi, this is a suggestion for people who don't have the option of someone power leveling them. The best place I have found to level from 8ish to 15ish is a small dungeon of the coast of Tou Tou called Smugler's Cove.

The place is filled with Banderlings of many levles. They are spaced out enough to where it usually isn't a problem for a low level character because you can fight them one-at-a-time. After you make your way to the bottom, you'll enter the last room. You will have a chest in front of you, a surface portal to the left, and to the right if you run all the way, in the corner is a hidden room. Go down the passageway and a Banderling Captain waits for you, plus 2 chests with noob sell items in them.

Rocky Crypt (50.9S, 69.3E) 
Recommended Levels: 10-20

Creatures: Sandstone and Granite Golems

It's a great place to pass from 10th level to 15th level, at which time you can run over to the convenient, located close by, Lin Luggie Citadel. There are 8-10 Sandstone Golems spread throughout and 2 Granite Golems that hang out in a back room. One of the main rooms has a Revitalize Fountain. It's a great place to get your mote count up for that upcoming Atlan weapon and all the level III jewelry that you can shake a stick at. Believe me, that's a lot of shaking.

Lo Tyreal of HG
Level 14 Sword

"Golem Valley", the cleft in the Mts. between Baishi and Lin 
Recommended Levels: 15+

Creatures: Golems (Limestone, Sandstone, Granite, occasionally Ice, Iron and Copper on random spawns, one Obsidian); Liches; Lugians (Laigus through Lithos, rarely a Gigas); various Matties, Dillos, Mossies, Gromnies, Linvak Ursuins and Shadows.

You have two Golem dungeons close by each other for Mote collection, a tower containing an Obsidian Golem and several Liches and worse, and the valley floor itself often spawns the more challenging denizens of Dereth's mainland. True, you won't be facing the most challenging beasts if you are of a respectable level, but for sheer volume of kills in a short time, running back and forth between those two towns can net a person decent exp and a fair haul of loot!

Forbidden Crypts (6.5N, 3.0E)
Recommended Level: 8-20

Creatures: 1-2 Undead (500xp ea), 1-2 Zombies (1000xp ea), 1 Lich at end.

Undead used to have nasty spells and debuffs.  With the new Armor gems from our friendly Lugians, the magic bolts don't hurt quite as badly. The experience is very good, unless the dungeon is heavily hunted.  Much, much better than the 'standard' recommendation of the mountains or the Eastham/Mayoi beaches.  However, they are getting more and more popular, so try to go in the off-peak hours.

Zombies drop a lot of magic clothes, so these are also good places to get that magic fez of Cooking Mastery I or II.

These are very good locations for archers to level (4-14 in 8 hours of play) and good ones for war mages.

allenflame's mule of FF
3 School Archer

Great xp and if you can cast level 3 or 4 protects you do ok. Starting at level 15 Iron Golems and Liches will wield a lot of xp with low to medium risk. Watch out for the Irons at lower level's for when they crit, they crit hard and usually send you to the lifestone if your health is under 100. Once you can cast level 5 buffs, you should be hunting the Revs. They give 10k xp each and the spawn is perfectly timed for a solo player (well at least for me, but I'm comfortable in my fighting style against them).  I was able to start hunting them at a lower level because my starting stats were much like a mage's except for the coord.  You may be hitting them later on in level's if you're not casting 5's by level 25.  But I would not try them without level 5 protects; they like to throw level 5 and 6 war spells on top of a lot of drains.

Level 42 Duelist

Dungeon Muddy (27.1S, 71.0E) 
Recommended Level: 10-20

Creatures: Golems (Mud, Limestone, Sandstone), Wasps (Blue and Mire), Lesser Mu-Miyahs.

Archer and war mage dream dungeon. Also some decent drain spots for life mages. Everything here can be hunted well with ranged spells or arrows. Quite a few long hallways allow ranged hunting. Lots of stamina fountains scattered about.

Golems drop gems and spell components (and a very occasional mote). Mu-miyahs drop magical jewelry and an occasional tiny mnemosyne. Wasps drop wings (duh). In other words, great loot for folks with low strength.

Lugian Tower (59.2S, 27.8E) 
Recommended Level: 10-20 Life mage (skill 115+)

Creatures: Lugians! (and Black Rats)  2 Laigus, 2 Amploth, 2 Obeloth and 1 Lithos

You might need someone to clear the way into the tower.  Once in go to the 2nd highest flight of stairs.  Don't go out on the ledge or stay too close to the door unless you like flying rocks.  Black Rats spawn above and sometimes fall onto the steps with you.  Don't kill the Black Rats to avoid this annoyance.  Take lots of comps as you may find it tough getting in and out of the tower.  By level 20 the Lugians will only yield 40K xp per hour. The tower is only good if you don't have to share it.
