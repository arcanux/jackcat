Preparations For The Kill

As a fun change of pace and so that Danier could practice some of his new Level VII spells, Ned Cleversun and I went PK! We made our preparations, muling our favorite items, just in case. I hauled out some old Celdon Leggings and my old Gold Coat, and Ned found an old green helmet to wear. After stopping by a convenient lifestone, we met up with Danier in Glendon Wood and headed out, our hearts light with blood-thirst and mayhem!

We took a couple of Broken Sigils from Dungeon Nye 6.2N x 34.4E to Grilhud the Hermit at 11.8N x 33.7E. She opened a portal to Old Talisman 2.3S x 85.6W in the Dires for us, and we hit the altar after fighting our way through the Undead, Zombies and Liches.

Broken Sigil
Value 7
Burden 50

 

Ned Screams Like A Girl

The brave Ned Cleversun, Level 60 creature killer extraordinaire, was unprepared for what came next! As he knelt before the altar, freeing himself from Asheron's protective magics and becoming one of Bael'Zharon's chosen, a demon, with hands full of fire, unfurled itself and spread its wings wide. Ned screamed like a girl. (j/k) ;-)


click on thumbnail to enlarge

THAT, of course, is the coolest animation in the game!  It was so cool that we clicked the altar again and again and again, until I'm sure the demon was ready to kill us himself. Eventually, we tore ourselves away and regrouped in GW. 

I found out that a mage with high melee defense was hard to kill. Maybe I could scratch him to death!

But then I hit upon a better idea!

 

Danier Buffs Maggie's Sword

That was certainly unexpected! Ned Cleversun crumpled to the ground in one blow from my mighty sword! Apparently Danier had just dropped Imperil VI on him! My first human kill and it was my very own vassal!!

I was full of remorse.

 

Danier Mocks Maggie

Emboldened by my early success I turned back to my original target and attacked Danier with renewed vigor and determination!

He was unimpressed.

 

Maggie Boasts & Brags

Still, mock as they  might, I was the only one of the three of us who had made a kill! I couldn't help boasting a little bit, rubbing it in. Ned took it badly.

He tried to pretend it was an accident!

 

Disturbing News

I knew, however, that it was no accident. When I returned, he was plinking away at Danier; the blood lust had clearly overtaken his better nature. My sweet vassal had been transformed into a hardened KILLER!! Turned out that Danier had dropped Blood Loather on his beloved composite bow and he was dead set on revenge! 

Then I received even more disturbing news. 

Only 4,000 points?? I became bitterly depressed. Doogen said I shouldn't fret. It's a very respectable number, comparable to an Olthoi Worker, even! These small attempts to cheer me could do nothing to lessen the heaviness on my spirit. Was I really worth so little? Fueled by raging fury and anguish, I took out my distress on Danier, who was lying helpless on the ground nearby, my mighty sword slashing through the air towards his prone figure.

Hee hee. I suck!

 

The Proper Attitude!

All in all, it was good to be a killer. I shared my joy with Veritus Venatus who gave me a few pointers in D00d Sp34K for future reference! 

I adjusted my attitude.

Since it was getting very late, we decided we had better head over to Trothyr's Rest to finish waiting out our 3 hours. We had a few moments of awkwardness as we fought our way through. We kept accidentally targeting each other, instead of the monsters! Danier showed up a spot at the top of a set of stairs where we could wait in relative safety.  I used some of my time doing spell research, trying to Master Recall the Sanctuary (where another Altar of Asheron resides). 


Was It All Just A Dream?

Coincidentally, news of a upcoming rollback reached our ears not too long after our night of killing. We did a little math, and figured out that the rollback would occur some time right in the middle of our PK adventure. We waited in anticipation! Secretly, I was hoping that the rollback would place us smack dab in the middle of our PK battle. Right at the time where I had killed Ned Cleversun, but he had not yet killed me! Bwahahaha! Or maybe we'd find out that we had not even gotten started. Had it all been just a dream? Or, worst of all, would Ned Cleversun have to sit out his grueling 3 hours for a 2nd time? You'll be glad to know that the rollback placed us right at the very tail end of our PK time. Our 3 hours had already expired and I was standing safely at the top of the stairs, right where they had left me! Would we do it again? You bet! Except next time... I'm bringing a Hollow Sword! ;-)

Tip: If you're going to play PK with a high level mage with melee defense, don't forget to bring your hollow weapons!
