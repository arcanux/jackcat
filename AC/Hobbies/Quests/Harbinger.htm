The following information is provided as a courtesy until I can research the quest for a full write-up.

The Harbinger originally appeared during the Verdict event. During Into the Darkness the quest was updated.

Dungeon: Tunnels to the Harbinger - 12.6S 46.7E (Levels 60+)
Text: Natural Order
Text: Experimentation
Quest Details: Harbinger
Timer: 3 Months

Chupon's Savoir, Emo II, Ussj Vin, Mystykal, Jaguar, Silenced, and Morgan Wulfson Jr just killed the Harbinger on LC.

Get flagged by talking to the virindi in Yanshi, retrieving one of the items from his book, and returning it to him. You are then flagged to enter.

The dungeon is straightforward. Just keep going down, hacking through rooms full of high level sclavi and tentacles. Be warned! They chain-harm and cast Life Bolts. LOTS of harms :) Also some dual-elementals and standard elementals. Nothing too scary.

There are a few simple jumps, just jump straight across, and watch where you're going to land. As far as we could tell, there's only one way through the dungeon, unless you count going for an acid bath as a viable route of travel ;)

Once you get to the Harbinger, he is surrounded by elementals. There's a reasonably safe buffing spot above him (just a few elementals that spawn up there), and you can debuff him and his cronies.

Once you jump down, you'd best have some folks in your group as dedicated healers. He hits hard and fast, and casts a lot of very nasty debuffs and wars. Quick trips to the lifestone for anyone that's not well prepared :)

Our group used Pierce, which seemed to work well. His magicd isn't very high - I was able to land on him with 340ish Life skill.

When he dies, he leaves behind a number of arms. Grab one and give it to Asheron's Emmisary outside for a "Champion of Dereth" title. Talking to the virindi will give you 1 point in the skill related to the token you gave him.

No word yet on how to help ol' Nuhmidura..

Cheers,
- Diatribe of Leafcull (Morgan Wulfson Jr)

Info on Jumps

I thought I would send this along to you, because my allegiance did the quest last night, and we had a lot of people either die or recall from the jumping.

So far on the quest write-up it just says ... "There are a few simple jumps, just jump straight across, and watch where you're going to land.

I thought this might be a little more enlightening for those still new to the quest and don't know what to expect like our group.

We all talked to Vincadi and went in that dungeon.

At the first jump the opening is in the middle of the wall. At the bottom is a fire pit. You can do a full jump, but keep the "forward key" pressed down - you will hit the wall and drag yourself into the hallway. It worked for me twice, but I am not sure if lag will mess it up. If you don't make the jump, there is no way out other than recalling.

The second jump our group didn't have a problem with both times. You just do a 1/4 power jump down to the North. If you do a full jump, you will over jump to the other side. If that happens it's not a problem, just quickly run back around facing to the North again. Although it is very hard to see in this area from all the effects.

The third jump incorporates 3 jumps. All will be to the North. The first jump you do a shift jump with about 1/4 power (this can be overjumped for dire consequences). The next jump can be done with full power and the same with the last jump. If you land in the fire pit on the last jump, you will have to recall out.

On the fourth jump you will want to do a shift jump with about 1/4 power, it is not as crucial, but it lines you up better to run thru the acid to the South. Once you make it thru the acid and in the hallway to the South - move away from the edge. We lost a couple people thinking they were safe just inside the hallway past the acid.

For a group new to the quest and doesn't know what to expect from the jumps I would recommend people jumping down one at a time, with a healer constantly healing them. The healer should be the last one down, but on the fourth jump beware of the respawning elementals. During the jumping the healer should be different from the usual healer(s), it should be someone with experience leading, jumping and that can improvise.

At the last jump before the Harbinger room, you can jump down safely and are able to vuln and debuff everything in the Harbinger room. 2-3 elementals will then spawn in this area, but its not too bad. Once they are all debuffed you can then run thru the fire and jump down into the Harbinger room for your final confrontation.
