Just prior to this month's even, Martine and Gaerlan engaged in a fierce battle atop Gaerlan's Citadel. Details of what happened are unclear, but it seems that just as Martine was about to fall, he unleashed a powerful spell that brought the citadel crashing down from the sky. The fallen citadel lies in a crater in the mountains of Northern Osteth. The fate of Martine remains unknown; only his robe and mask were found.

The menace Gaerlan, however, was still intent on his destructive path. Golems and elementals attacked the towns. As brave defenders struck back, four portals opened in Eastham.

In Eastham, four portals open. Created by Asheron, these portals lead to caverns inhabited by golems created to defend the accessways to Gaerlan's grounded citadel. Warriors must divide themselves and defeat these guardians to gain entrance to the portals in the valley of the fallen citadel.

The Guardians
Inside the caverns, you'll need to kill the Guardian Golems. When they are defeated, a portal to the Citadel opens and Asheron spawns. When all of the guardians are dead, the portals will disappear.

Asheron spawns in the crater by the fallen citadel at 57.7N, 48.4E. Speak to him for an Imbued Pyreal Nugget which allows you to enter the Ziggurat portal.

Note: Asheron only spawned the first time the quest was run. Now, a Platinum Golem will give you the nugget.

To reach Asheron by land, take the Colier View Hill portal at the Cragstone Settlement portals (26.3N, 46.3E). Run to 60.2N, 47.6E to cross over the mountain ridge, then run to 56.6N, 49.6E. Buff your jump to ~310 and do a full running power jump at an upward angle. You should land on the slope then you can work your way around the crater ridge from there. On the NE side of the crater, you'll see a dip. Do a medium shift jump down onto the snowy patch, then another jump down onto the ground. (No longer available.)

Note: If you log off inside the citadel crater, you will log back in at the lifestone. Also, do not attempt to jump on to the citadel itself; an invisible barrier will impede you.

The Ziggurats
Once you've received the nugget, you have been flagged to enter the Ziggurat at 57.2N, 47.8E. Choose the one that corresponds to your level.

Lightning Ziggurat - 20-39
Acid Ziggurat - 40-59
Fire Ziggurat - 60-79
Frost Ziggurat - 80+

The Lightning Ziggurat (20-39) has Lightning Elementals (Sparks, Synnasts and Voltarcs), level 83 Stasis Wisps and Granite Golems.

The Acid Ziggurat (40-59) has Strife Wisps, Coral Golems, and Acid Elementals, including Mox and Buillics.

The Fire Ziggurat (60-79) is full of Fire Elementals -- Flares, Flammas, Infernos, Hellfires, Corrosion Wisps and a few Diamond Golems. Go NW at the the first maze area, and west at the second.

The Frost Ziggurat (80+) has Frost Elementals, Tempest Wisps, Acidic Diamond Golems and Platinum Golems.

Each dungeon is similar in design, with a pre-maze area where you'll be fighting most of the creatures, a maze area (head up), and the end portion with the Platinum Golem.

When you reach the end, hand the Imbued Pyreal Nugget to the Golem and it will open a portal to the Proving Grounds.

If you answer it correctly, you will be portalled to the next area and given a Seal Fragment. If you answer incorrectly 3 times, you'll get an all expense paid trip to Teth!

Trail#2 -- Rolling Death
Next you will face Rolling Balls of Death! Yes, that's right: Rolling Balls of Death! Your task is to get to the end of the corridor, dodging the rolling death balls, and through the door. The door, however, is not an ordinary door! It must be smote! Melee attacks will be almost impossible. Instead, use archery to attack it from afar. Your corners will be safe spots. Because of the door's low resist, even players without bow trained can be effective with a store bought bow or crossbow and some arrows. Once the door has been defeated, the balls of death will stop spawning temporarily. In the following chamber, you'll find chests with more seal fragments, and a portal to the next area of the proving ground.

Map: Rolling Death Safe Spots Map
(Thanks to Sangre Fria of SC!)

Trail#3 -- Floor Puzzle
The third task is a floor puzzle. To make your way across, you'll need to use a diagram that can be found in one of the previous corridors. Don't try to jump across; there is an invisible ceiling. At the other side, you must hit a switch which causes the chests with the seal fragments to spawn. A portal to the final area will also open.

Map: Floor Puzzle Map
(Thanks to Archmage Hunter of FF!)

Trail#4 -- Reverence of the Ceremony
For your final task, you must show your patience and discipline by passing through a room in silence. No talking, and no spellcasting. If you quietly walk through, you will be safe. If you speak, you will be portalled back to the start of the dungeon. Once through the room, pull the switch for your final seal fragment and take the portal back to Asheron. Two golems may also spawn in this room. Melees may fight them if necessary.

The seal fragments fit together to create part of a larger seal. There is still a section missing.

The Hearths
Combine the Seal Fragments into an Ornate Seal. Hand it to Asheron and in return, he will return an Imbued Ornate Seal and a Citadel Portal Gem. Use it to enter the Citadel. Inside, there are 4 Hearth portals with levels corresponding to the Ziggurat and Proving Grounds Portals.

You give Asheron Ornate Seal.
Asheron tells you, "Yes, I had thought the architecture was reminiscent of that epoch...You will need this for there are hidden pillars on the surface of the citadel. When you have destroyed the hearth gems they will be revealed."
Asheron tells you, "You must prepare yourself well for what you will face. On the surface of the citadel there are four large gems. Each functions as a small well between worlds, it is difficult to explain but think of it as a space within a space. There you will find one of the hearth gems that continue to draw mana from the ley lines of the world. They are fueling the spell which hides the pillars at the moment."
Asheron tells you, "I have constructed gems that will portal you onto the citadel. There you should find entrances into the hearth gems. Defeat the device that is within and then find the pillar. Once you have placed the seal within the pillar you should transported into the lowest level of the sanctum, located in the tower."
Asheron tells you, "I wish you well on your endeavor."
Asheron gives you Imbued Ornate Seal.
Asheron gives you Citadel Portal Gem.

Inside you'll find Hearth Crystals (level 999) which must be destroyed. The Fire Hearth Crystal (80+) has 400,000 health and the Ice Hearth Crystal (60-79) has 300,000 health points. The Hearth Crystals differ from the Elementals Cores in that they have low resistance and a very slow regen rate. You should be able to kill them without too much trouble.

The Library
An exit portal will appear after the Hearth Crystal is destroyed and you'll be back at the Citadel. The pillar will now be revealed. Give the pillar the Imbued Ornate Seal and you'll be portalled to the library. Climb the stairs and find three bookshelves. One gives you 5 books, another one book and the last a local broadcast. While you're wandering in the library, you'll hear whispers in your head. A list of library sayings can be found on the Verdict patch page. The books can be translated in Cragstone. Repeating the Quest
After the defeat of Gaerlan and his imprisonment, the quest may still be repeated, but instead of the successive aspect spawns, four level restricted portals await at the top of the library.

Ice Demise (20-39)
Fire Aspect Dungeon (40-59)
Acid Aspect Dungeon (60-79)
Extreme Electrical Dungeon of Destruction (80+)

Gaerlan is weak to Slashing Attacks. In order to get your prizes at the end, make sure each person has a chance to get a critical hit on Gaerlan. Srand, one of the live team Devs, stated on the boards that there are other ways than a war spell for mages to get flagged as completing a crit, but at this time, details are unknown.

Each group can do their section at their own pace. As Gaerlan's aspect falls in each section, a global message goes out:

[Character name] has defeated Gaerlan's [elemental] aspect. As the illusion crumbles away a deep voice thunders over the world, "A doomed eternity to relive this life. Stasis within the walls of a crystalling prison. How could Ihave fallen toyou vermin. Nuhmudira, my revenge upon you shall be as painful as yours upon me."

A portal then opens to the Inner Sanctum. You'll run over a fire and acid pit, them jump down. You should then see Gaerlan, trapped, floating above the pillar. Double click on the pillar for your reward!

The player who got credit for killing Gaerlan will receive the Black Robe. All others will receive a Red Rob, Gaerlan's Sword (for display only), and one of the other items. At this time, it is unknown what causes a player to receive a particular prize. The best guess so far is that it's based on skill credits spent. For example, War is the most expensive skill at 16 skill credits, so if you have War Trained, you'll get the War Wand. As you go through successive times, you will get the prize associated with your next highest expensive skill. (Thus the plethora of War and Life Wands, and the Shield). Weapons may be dropped or given away. Robes are no drop, no give.

Timer is "a full cycle of days." (A week.)

Once you have done the quest enough times to get all the items you can (depending on which skills you have trained), you will get the following message:

Gaerlan's Phylacteric Prison gives you Elemental Master Robe.
Gaerlan's Phylacteric Prison gives you Iasparailaun.
A woman's voice sounds in your head, "There is nothing left of his soul that you can take, you have stripped him of all that he possesses that would be of use to you."
