A report from Gandalf Zu'l Zoran of SC: "Hiya Maggie, I followed up on a town crier rumor nd headed to the Dires Great Lighthouse at 25.8S, 46.9W there you will find Hermit. When you approach Hermit you get the following:"

The hermit's eyes grow wide as he begins to speak.
Lighthouse Hermit tells you, "Ah, I do remember now. I didn't think it would take this long, but no matter - you are here. If you will but listen to my story, I will reveal a great secret to you."
Lighthouse Hermit tells you, "I once was the greatest and most feared arbalister in all of Dereth. I once had everything a man could want from this life, and I have enjoyed all that I have experienced over the long years."
Lighthouse Hermit tells you, "But decades of adventure and excitement have caught up finally, I'm afraid. I find myself unable to see the bull's-eye any longer, thanks to the slow dimming of my sight. And more often than not I find the string to be too powerful for these feeble hands to draw or load."
Lighthouse Hermit tells you, "However, a ghost of the past still stirs in this old body. And before death's final arrow tears through the dark night in search of this weary target, I must see to it that my secret becomes known -- it is with you I entrust this knowledge."
 The old hermit shifts his position, and begins to talk with a slow and deliberate voice.
Lighthouse Hermit tells you, "I have seen with my own eyes the devastating power of magic; how easily a burning fireball can send the most skilled archer to his demise!"
Lighthouse Hermit tells you, "It has long been said: "The only way to combat magic is with stronger magic." Before you sits an old man who has come to understand what this truly means."
Lighthouse Hermit tells you, "I obtained certain artifacts and studied about old, forgotten magic. I devised for myself a way to empower my crossbow with a powerful, arcane device - a fetish, one might call it."
Lighthouse Hermit tells you, "Any missile weapon may be empowered similarly. Be warned - the magic is strong, and thus irreversible by any process I know of. I will tell you all I can remember soon enough. But today I have talked long enough."
Lighthouse Hermit tells you, "Travel now to Aerlinthe Island. Find the old ones, the ancient diamond behemoths roaming that enchanted, volcanic isle. Retrieve the ancient diamond relic, and return to me. I should more clearly see the past when I am again holding a piece of it."

Lighthouse Hermit tells you, "You must find this ring, for it contains a most vital magical element."

You allow Lighthouse Hermit to examine your Warding Ring.
The hermit rolls the ring back and forth in his hand slowly, thinking deeply about the past.
Lighthouse Hermit tells you, "A warding ring..."
Lighthouse Hermit tells you, "Long ago I took a ring identical to this one from the charred and burning hand of a fiend. That time seems unreal to me now, beyond my grasp in memory."
Lighthouse Hermit tells you, "I do remember some of the important details, though. This ring conceals a small glass vial containing blood. You will need this foul fluid."
Lighthouse Hermit tells you, "There is no way for either of us to remove the vial from the ring, I'm afraid. But there is one who can -- one who knows of the darker magics."

I returned to Hermit:

You allow Lighthouse Hermit to examine your Vial of Black Blood.
Lighthouse Hermit tells you, "So you have been to the seer's jungle abode? You seem to be handling all of this adventure and excitement quite easily."
Lighthouse Hermit tells you, "Hold onto this vial as if your life depended on it. The blood will be useful...but I am having trouble remembering in what way."

You allow Lighthouse Hermit to examine your Infected Assailer Fur.
Lighthouse Hermit tells you, "Oh, what an awful smell! I remember this stench more than ever! I hope the experience did not make you too ill."
The old hermit thinks hard for a few moments.
Lighthouse Hermit tells you, "I remember...I took the fur somewhere, and it was fashioned into a sturdy piece of rope, or cord if you will."
Lighthouse Hermit tells you, "I am sorry, but I cannot recall how this was accomplished. You will have to figure out a way to bind or weave this fur."

Take the Fur to Neydisa Castle and use it on the Loom you will get:

You give Loom Infected Assailer Fur.
You feed the infected assailer fur into the loom, hoping it does not break the machine.
The loom whines loudly, and continues to weave the fur.
The loom produces a thick, braided cord of tusker fur.
Loom gives you Woven Assailer Fur.

You allow Lighthouse Hermit to examine your Woven Assailer Fur.
Lighthouse Hermit tells you, "Ah, you've done it -- I knew you would find a way!

Use the blood on the Woven Fur:

Suddenly a thought bursts into your mind: "The blood will strengthen the bond." Had Kleeoh said this?
Lighthouse Hermit tells you, "I remember now! The braided tusker fur is physically strong, indeed -- however it must be magically strong as well, otherwise it is useless."
You pour the black blood all over the woven tusker fur.
You allow Lighthouse Hermit to examine your Blood-soaked Cord.
The hermit pulls on the ends of the blood-soaked cord, testing it's toughness.
Lighthouse Hermit tells you, "You could not have done better! This braided cord, soaked in the black blood of the fiends, will serve as a bridge between the two idol guardians. It will also bind the powerful magic of the idols to your weapon."
Lighthouse Hermit tells you, "Speaking of idols, you will be pleased to know there is only one more relic to obtain."
Lighthouse Hermit tells you, "The diamond relic will provide magical protection, but used alone it is heavy and unbalancing. You would hardly be able to steady your weapon with only one idol hanging from it!"
Lighthouse Hermit tells you, "A similar idol is necessary not only to balance the weight of the first idol, but to balance it's protective powers as well."
Lighthouse Hermit tells you, "It is getting late, so I will not keep you any longer. You must make your way to Marae Lassel, and retrieve a relic from the Great Elariwood golems that inhabit the wild northern forests of that island."
Lighthouse Hermit tells you, "You will know it when you see it. An elariwood idol is very similar to a diamond idol in appearance. Find it and return to me."

Alien Killer of SC reports on where to find Great Elariwood Idols:

From the Subway, take the Holtburg portal and go to the West Outpost, where you are going to find a Redsphire (spelling?) portal. Enter Redsphire and follow the rode to where you would turn in Pincers. There is a North Plains Portal there, take that. Beware: If you cant handle groups of lvl 70-110 monsters, don't enter. Once your in, run towards the cliff and find the strip of land that is like a forest. Run up and down the strip untill you find a group (usually 3) of Great Elariwood Golems.

Fetish of the Dark Idols
This bizarre creation seems to pulse in your hands, sending powerful ripples of energy through your arms.
Combine this with any loot-generated atlatl, bow, or crossbow. The Fetish of the Dark Idols will apply a Magic Absorbing property and a Melee Defense penalty to the weapon. Although the weapon can be imbued before applying a Fetish of the Dark Idols, it cannot be imbued afterwards. The weapon may have non-imbue tinkers applied either before or after application of the Fetish of the Dark Idols.
This item cannot be sold.
