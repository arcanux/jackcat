Sometimes you just want to kick back and relax, put away the sword, and ponder life's ineffable mysteries. And what better way to do that than by fishing!! To begin, grab a C Note (no pyreals!), a Wood Golem Heart or Golem Jo, and your patience, and head to one of the new Tackle Masters.

Cragstone Waterfall - 29.0N, 43.9E
Shoushi Waterfront - 32.1S, 74.2E
Holtburg Waterfront - 43.0N, 34.1E
Yaraq Waterfront - 22.0S, 2.2W
Nanto Waterfall- 52.6S, 80.8E
Rithwic Bridge - 10.7N, 58.7E

Tackle Master tells you, "Oh, I also supply the local grocers with fresh fish, so I'll buy your catch from you if you like. I pay a lot more than most grocers will, because I know the fish are fresh. Good luck and remember to throw the small ones back."

Once you've received your pole in exchange for the heart or Jo, and your license for the C Note (good for 24 hours), you're ready to cast! Find an unoccupied fishing hole (one of the blue dots on your radar) and throw a line into the water (equip the pole in your weapon slot and double click the hole or use your "R" key).

As you fish, your skill will increase. (To see your skill, click on the burden icon in the upper right of your screen.) At the lowest skill, you'll catch Minnows and Guppies. As you become more proficient, you'll begin catching Mollies, Fish, and finally Large Fish! A nearby sign also indicates that when you catch a specific number of fish, you can receive a Title Token for a new Title!

Below is a list of the approximate fishing skill needed to catch the next size fish.

Minnows  - Fishing Skill 0
Guppies  - Fishing Skill 0
Mollies  - Fishing Skill 50
Fish  - Fishing Skill 125
Large Fish  - Fishing Skill 225

You can also increase your skill by releasing fish back into the fishing hole. The easiest way to do this is by mapping a "Give Selected Item" key. Catching Minnows will not increase your skill; however, as you become a more proficient fisher, you can gain points by catching certain colors of the larger fish (see tables to the right).

Once caught, fish can be sold to the Tackle Master for pyreal (see table to right for values), or they can be released back into the water. If your fish are in good condition when they are released, you can gain even more skill points!

Note: Fish are stackable, but since they must be turned in one at a time, you probably won't want to stack them. If your backpack is full, you will not be able to accept the fish or the skill points.

Timing is everything! To catch fish larger than Minnows, your best success comes by timing your cast when the fish are jumping. Once you figure out the timing of your particular fishing hole, you can get a point almost every time! There are two animations for fishie movement, a high jump and a quick twirl which happens just under the surface. If your hook is in the water during one of the animations, you'll catch a point giving fish, as long as you have the skill for it!

In addition to fish, you may also catch trinkets or old junk, such as jewelry, clothing or weapons. The rusted items are particularly desirable, as they can be salvaged! You'll receive 6 units of the following salvage materials:

Rusted Battle Axe - Copper
Rusted Dirk - Pyreal
Rusted Mace - Iron
Rusted Medalion - Gold
Rusted Nekode - Bronze
Rusted Shouken - Brass
Rusted Tachi - Steel
Rusted Trident - Silver

Other odd items, such as an Old Boot, a Trunk of Bones, a Ball of Gunk or a Skeleton have been pulled from the waters. The rusted items and other retrieved items may be placed on housing hooks.
