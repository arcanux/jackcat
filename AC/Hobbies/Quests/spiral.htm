Visited Forth Tethana, found Lieutenant Durgan inside the tavern.

Lieutenant Durgan tells you, "Greetings, friend. Though we do not claim independence...yet...we still must look after our own concerns, since Elysa Strathelar won't. At times, we look to outsiders for aid. Are you willing to be that aid we seek?"
The lieutenant shrugs his shoulders.
Lieutenant Durgan tells you, "I am Commander Margyle's intelligence chief, and I have a very vexing problem. It seems that Elysa sent her army in our direction, no doubt to put us directly under her heel again. A Viamontian force also made passage from Marae Lassel, and all projections indicated that they would fight each other north of here, near the coast."
Lieutenant Durgan tells you, "No matter who won, we would have gained an advantage. But what I find odd is this..."
Lieutenant Durgan tells you, "There was also word of a Renegade Tumerok band mobilizing to engage the other two forces. The three armies apparently encamped and parlayed, but there is no word from any of the forces after that. No army can keep themselves so hidden, especially not on our territory."
Lieutenant Durgan tells you, "I want to know what happened to those three armies, and I will reward you to tell me. If you are truly helpful, I may discuss what the future holds for beloved Tethana.

Run due north til you get to a huge black scorched area filled with shadows and drudges, looks like a lot are dressed in yellow and red cov armor.
Running around and you will find lots of traces of the viamontian, the tumerok and the strathelar army.

Details from Q Manguy of MT:

Ok Maggie. I was unable to complete this quest on my first attempt! But here’s what I got! Town criers say that Lieutenant Allandal on Plateau Village needs volunteers, and mentions NOT Lord Marson and previously. If you go to Plateau Village the small army group is now GONE!! Only Lt. Allandal is present. Here is what she says:

Lieutenant Allandal tells you, "Peace be with you in these dark times. I serve Queen Elysa Strathelar in an information gathering role, but currently those of us who gather information for Her Majesty are baffled by a strange occurance."
Lieutenant Allandal tells you, "We recently sent an army to the Fort Tethana region, a move that was matched by the Viamontian loyalists. We have heard nothing about either force since a report that they had sat down to parlay, except for a rumor of Tumerok activity in the area."
Lieutenant Allandal tells you, "The Queen would like you to explore the area, and return with any information you find."
Lieutenant Allandal tells you, "I have heard that others are in search of information about what happened to the forces. Although Her Majesty would prefer that people aid her, in this matter she believes that something which transcends this war has occurred. As such, if you choose to aid the Viamontians, there will be no negative ramifications from our side."
Lieutenant Allandal tells you, "Also, if you have ventured to the fortifications between here and Fort Tethana, I will accept any Viamontian banners you may have retrieved."

So I ran out to Fort Teth. I found a new NPC in Teth’s bar as well! Lieutenant Durgan! He says he is an independent party in this. He does not side with Queen Elysa or King Varicci.

Lieutenant Durgan tells you, "Greetings, friend. Though we do not claim independence...yet...we still must look after our own concerns, since Elysa Strathelar won't. At times, we look to outsiders for aid. Are you willing to be that aid we seek?"
The lieutenant shrugs his shoulders.
Lieutenant Durgan tells you, "I am Commander Margyle's intelligence chief, and I have a very vexing problem. It seems that Elysa sent her army in our direction, no doubt to put us directly under her heel again. A Viamontian force also made passage from Marae Lassel, and all projections indicated that they would fight each other north of here, near the coast."
Lieutenant Durgan tells you, "No matter who won, we would have gained an advantage. But what I find odd is this..."
Lieutenant Durgan tells you, "There was also word of a Renegade Tumerok band mobilizing to engage the other two forces. The three armies apparently encamped and parlayed, but there is no word from any of the forces after that. No army can keep themselves so hidden, especially not on our territory."
Lieutenant Durgan tells you, "I want to know what happened to those three armies, and I will reward you to tell me. If you are truly helpful, I may discuss what the future holds for beloved Tethana."

Durgan said that Viamontian and Queen Elysa’s soldiers were going to parley on the North coast north of Fort Teth. So I ran north. Near the coast I found a large black area with LOTS of shadow. It’s basically a crater with a plateau in the middle of it. In the crater I found new Shadow types. It appears that Strathelar’s soldiers, and Viamontian soldiers have been turned into Shadow! They are still weak to Fire! If you look around you’ll also find penants laying on the ground and dead bodies. Looks like Shadow destroyed everyone on both sides, plus the Tumerok forces that tried to ambush them. There are L125-135 Shadow Wraiths in the crater. LOTS of them so take friends with you, as they cast War and Life Drains, as well as melee damage.

On top of the plateau I found a portal called “The Dark Spiral” (16.1N 68.9W). It is restricted to L126+, is NOT recallable or tie-able. Once you get inside the drop point IS SAFE for rebuffing as my group had to. Our group was 3 mages, L156, L167, L174. They didn’t name this place a spiral for nothing. The dungeon seems to be a maze almost. If you go straight down the spiral you encounter L161 Twisted Shadow. Normal versions, Viamontian, and Strathelar versions. All weak to fire. I also later ran into Barbaric Mukkir which are L180. They were also weak to fire. Note: They do melee and War, and seem prone to cold attacks. After taking a L7 Cold debuff I was hit for 157 by a Mukkir.

We found our way to a 3 way split. We decided to go straight. Later we found a 2 way split. We went right. It just kept going down until we eventually found ourselves going up again. I thought we were lost. We came to more splits again, and I think we kept going left. Eventually we found ourselves going down again until we found a pit. Lots of orange dots below us. I found 2-3 The Black Breaths, L648. Several L161 Shadow, and at least 2 of the Barbaric Mukkir (which resist a lot). When I hit my [ ] keys I was able to target something called the “Lore of the Black Spiral” down below us somewhere. We tried to shoot everything in the pit before we jumped but found it very difficult because everything kept moving. When we thinned it out we finally jump. But shortly there after things began to spawn again! I also noted throughout our journey 2 other people had died in the dungeon ahead of us…. Eeek.

One of my fellows got cornered and was killed. At this point realizing things were spawning again I ran circles around the small square pit. I could NOT see any exits. Perhaps because it was all dark and black, and there was smoke/fog in the room. Or perhaps I was scared. I did note however that before we jumped into the pit, monsters could run beneath us as if maybe there is actually a false wall. I couldn’t find it before a Mukkir killed me, and then instantly killed the 3rd party member as well. A L200 melee allegiance member of mine was also solo’ing the dungeon as we died. She died about 5 minutes after we did. THIS IS DEFINITELY A GROUP QUEST DUNGEON.

That’s all I have for right now. I’ve attached a few screenshots for you. I’m not sure who to talk to if you wish to side with Viamontian on this quest yet…

Q Manguy (Morningthaw)

Ok Maggie. Couple more bits of detail for you. Hehe, I took 2 fellows of people with me into the dungeon this time. We faired well but 1 person died because he was impatient and ran off away from the group.

Attached are Pictures of TWO of the possible rewards.

The Lore of the Dark Spiral (it’s a book) spawns on the floor against the WEST wall in the pit at the bottom of the dungeon. No idea how we got there, took over an hour. All I can say is, once you get REAL directions, people had better follow them or they WILL run in circles!!

If you turn in the book to Lieutenant Allandal you get the Shield of the Strathelar Elite! AL 300, buffable, and has Moderate Impregnability on it. Wield req of 126+… plus 28.4mil xp

If you turn the book into Lt. Durgan in Fort Teth, you get… tada! Heh… ‘Tethana Manifesto’ You can turn it back in for more xp but I didn’t do that. You get 28.4mil xp for handing in the book. The Manifesto, if hooked to your house will cast “Will of the People” on you which is a Stackable Self buff. HOWEVER, it WILL NOT stack with Major Willpower. Yeah, so for most upper level folks it’s probably a waste. ;-)

Prior to completing the quest he gives the following response.

Lieutenant Dialossa tells you, "Let me be honest. Your allegiance, whether for or against His Majesty, doesn't matter for the particular matter I am dealing with. Frankly, it seems like a threat against all Isparians."
Lieutenant Dialossa tells you, "The two warring sides were on the brink of battle, at long last, near the northern coast of the Direlands. But Count D'Orza has not sent a report back to King Varicci. Neither have the Bloodless sent a report back to their leader. There was a detachment of Tumeroks in the area as well, and they also have not been heard from since."

Tethana Manifesto
The people of Tethana resolve the following: That the relationship between a monarch and their vassals is one of obligation for both parties; That upon the inability of one party to fulfill their obligation, the other party may terminate their obligation and not be considered forsworn; That the chief duty of a leader is to protect and nurture all people within their domain; That the chief duty of a follower is to give obedience to their leader; That a follower is obligated, should the leader be unable to perform their duty, to break from their leader and to establish a new leader. -Arwyth Margyle
If this item is hooked, it may be used to cast Will of the People, a Self spell which stacks with normal spells.
