 During the Castling event, Spell Research came to an end. Instead of testing combinations of components, trying to hit on the right formula, NPC Scriveners began to sell scrolls (levels 1-6). Your Arcane Lore skill determines your ability to learn the spells from the scrolls. Even if you Arcane Lore skill is high, you still might not be able to read the scroll if your skill in that school of magic is too low. In the old days, players would carry around a scroll and would examine it every 15 minutes or so to gain a little xp for their Arcane Lore skill.

An alternative to spell components was also introduced during the Castling event. Each school of magic has a foci which you can purchase from the Scriveners to use for your spells in place of spell components. Foci must be used with Prismatic Tapers and they take up a slot in your backpack. If you're a four school mage, you will carry 4 Foci, taking up 4 of your available packs. Some mages prefer to still use the old fashioned method of carrying separate spell components in favor of pack space over lesser burden. Scriveners have an incredibly high mark-up rate; foci cost 25,000p each!

Of course, an old time-honored way for obtaining scrolls is by looting them off creatures you kill.
